# Luxury Segment: Divergence from Middle-Market

Status: stub. Not deepened in Phase 1. This file enumerates how luxury (Class-A /
lifestyle) is expected to differ from `middle_market`.

The luxury segment includes urban Class-A, lifestyle suburban Class-A, branded and
concierge-tier properties where the resident is paying for service and experience
as much as for the unit itself.

<a id="service_divergence"></a>

## Service standards divergence

- Lead response is substantially compressed. Luxury residents expect near-immediate
  human contact during business hours.
- Tour experience is choreographed: private tours, amenity demos, concierge
  introductions. Middle-market's self-guided or walk-in posture does not apply.
- Work-order SLAs are tighter at every priority tier. P3 routine repairs in
  middle-market may be same-day in luxury.
- Amenity cleanliness cadence is significantly higher (pool, fitness, lounge,
  rooftop, package concierge, pet spa).
- On-site concierge or front-desk staffing implies a staffing ratio and training
  posture not present in middle-market.

<a id="finish_divergence"></a>

## Finish standards divergence

- Full quartz or stone counters, premium appliance package (stainless steel, often
  gas in markets where available), hardwood or premium LVP, smart-home controls,
  premium lighting and plumbing fixtures.
- Closet systems, balcony finishes, premium window treatments as part of the
  base-unit package.
- Common-area design: curated art, designer furniture, branded signage. Maintenance
  cost per SF on common areas is meaningfully higher than middle-market.
- Brand consistency matters; finish deviation requires brand-standard approval
  rather than the middle-market "like-for-like" rule.

<a id="concession_divergence"></a>

## Concession posture divergence

- Concessions are brand-protecting. A free-month framing is sometimes preferred
  over a percent-off-rent framing to preserve headline rent.
- Move-in credit and lifestyle incentives (hotel stay, moving service,
  lifestyle-partner credits) substitute for direct discounts in some markets.
- Renewal concessions are more exceptional than in middle-market because
  retention hinges on service experience more than on price.

## Amenity program divergence

- Amenity density is materially higher (multiple lounges, coworking, rooftop,
  curated fitness, pet program, wellness room, entertainment kitchen, wine cellar
  in some markets, theater or golf simulator in branded properties).
- Amenity programming (events, classes, concierge services) is part of the
  retention engine and adds a programming vendor stack not present in
  middle-market.
- Package management is a first-class service surface, not a side task.

## Staffing divergence

- Concierge, resident services, and event programming roles exist.
- Hospitality training for leasing and service staff; higher training hours per
  role than middle-market.
- Maintenance staff skew more specialized (smart-home, high-end appliance,
  specialty HVAC).

## Vendor mix divergence

- Premium finish vendors, curated service providers (window washing, elevator
  maintenance with shorter SLAs, pest control to high-touch cadence), brand-
  standard appliance service.
- Event and programming vendors (caterers, fitness instructors, hospitality
  partners).
- Insurance and license verification applies identically; guardrails do not relax.

## Communication divergence

- Tone remains respectful and plain-language, but register shifts toward
  hospitality-brand voice.
- Channel posture emphasizes concierge interaction; automated communications are
  supplements, not substitutes.
- Luxury residents expect proactive communication about amenity events,
  service-area updates, and elevated personalization within fair-housing limits.

## Reporting divergence

- Retention rate, NPS or equivalent resident satisfaction, amenity utilization, and
  service-recovery rate join the primary emphases.
- Concession and trade-out are present but framed against headline-rent-protection
  posture.

## What Phase 1 does not build

- Full overlay.yaml depth.
- Brand-standard finish libraries (these are org-specific).
- Concierge staffing and programming packs.
- Amenity-program vendor mix and cadence.

These remain on the roadmap after middle-market depth stabilizes.
