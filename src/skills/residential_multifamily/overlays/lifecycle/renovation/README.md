# Renovation Lifecycle Overlay

Applies during active in-place value-add: classic-to-renovated unit programs,
common-area renovation, and amenity upgrades. Loads alongside the stabilized
lifecycle overlay when the property is operating and renovating simultaneously.

## What this overlay shifts

- Reporting emphasis adds: renovation yield on cost, scope-cost variance, classic-
  to-renovated throughput, turn-to-renovation conversion rate, capex-spend-vs-plan.
- Resident-experience posture tilts toward proactive communication and faster
  response during disruption windows (noise, dust, temporary amenity closures).
- Finish package invoked is the segment's value-add package (see
  `overlays/segments/middle_market/finish_standards.md`).
- Vendor requirements tighten: scope-qualified, license-verified, insurance-current,
  schedule-reliable.
- Work-order triage during active renovation distinguishes unit-in-progress from
  units outside the program; unit-in-progress requests route to the renovation
  vendor or GC superintendent rather than to standard site maintenance.

<a id="resident_comm_package"></a>

## Resident communication package

- Pre-start notice: describe the program, expected disruption windows, common-area
  impact, and point of contact. Plain-language voice per
  `overlays/segments/middle_market/resident_comm_tone.md`.
- Weekly progress update during active renovation phases.
- Noise / dust window advisories with at least the notice period required by the
  jurisdiction (jurisdictional review required per guardrails).
- Completion notice with any resident-visible changes to access, amenities, or
  services.

<a id="scope_cost_review"></a>

## Scope-cost variance review

- Variance by CSI division or trade vs. approved scope.
- Change orders tied to `_core/approval_matrix.md` rows 10 and 11.
- Yield-on-cost refresh at each renovation milestone; break-points for continuing,
  pausing, or re-scoping the program are documented in the approved capital plan.
