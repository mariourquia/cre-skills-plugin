# Recap-Support Lifecycle Overlay

Applies when the asset is preparing for a refinance, disposition, partnership
buyout, or recapitalization. Loads alongside the stabilized lifecycle overlay; the
recap overlay layers outward-facing readiness on top of ongoing operations.

## What this overlay shifts

- Reporting emphasis: NOI trend, T-12 normalization, rent-roll integrity, market
  comp package, narrative framing for lenders or buyers.
- Notice-exposure ceiling tightens during marketing and diligence windows.
- Renewal strategy tilts to deliver a favorable expiration-ladder shape and WALT.
- Approval posture leans on asset-manager, portfolio-manager, and finance leader;
  any lender or investor submission marked `final` is gated per approval matrix.

<a id="rent_roll_scrub"></a>

## Rent-roll scrub

- Reconcile rent roll to GL revenue for the trailing period.
- Validate concession schedules, lease expirations, square footage, and unit
  statuses.
- Identify and remediate mis-coded concessions, stale unit statuses, unreconciled
  receivables.
- Output is a clean rent roll plus reconciliation notes suitable for a data room.

<a id="comp_package_prep"></a>

## Comp-package preparation

- Market comp set assembled per the segment's comp methodology (not embedded
  here).
- Lease comps across new-lease and renewal distribution, aged within the window
  defined in reference.
- Trade-out and loss-to-lease positioning for the buyer's or lender's model.

<a id="disposition_narrative"></a>

## Disposition / refi narrative

- Value-creation story: what the owner did, what remains, what the next owner or
  next loan supports.
- Risk disclosures: outstanding capex, material deferred maintenance, tenant
  concentration, lease exposure.
- Renewal and trade-out trajectory.
- Environmental, zoning, and title status summarized at a level appropriate for
  the document type (OM, lender package, partner memo).

## Guardrail notes
