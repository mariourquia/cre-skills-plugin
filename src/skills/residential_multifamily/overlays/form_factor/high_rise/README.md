# High-Rise Form Factor (Stub)

Status: stub. Not deepened in Phase 1.

High-rise properties are nine or more stories. Compared with mid-rise, they add:

- Multiple elevator banks with dedicated service elevator. Elevator maintenance
  contracts are more complex and carry tighter SLAs.
- Fire-life-safety systems at scale: standpipe, sprinkler riser, smoke-evac, fire
  pump, fire command center. Testing cadence and licensed-work requirements
  expand.
- Facade maintenance and window washing programs. Swing-stage or rope-access
  vendors with specialized licensing.
- Stacked-unit plumbing risers and longer waste runs, creating distinctive leak
  and odor surfaces.
- Larger amenity programs with rooftop, sky-lounge, and often multi-level amenity
  floors.
- Concierge and front-desk staffing standard.
- Loading-dock and freight-elevator logistics for move-ins, turns, and vendor
  access.
- Tower-management coordination when the residential tower is one of several uses
  on a podium.

## Placeholder divergence

- Common-area maintenance cadence is higher density (elevator banks, rooftop,
  sky-lounges, fitness, spa).
- Staffing includes concierge and front-desk; maintenance is more specialized.
- Vendor mix skews specialty (elevator, facade, fire-life-safety).
- Turn scope adds loading-dock staging logistics.
- Work-order SLAs often tighter due to cross-floor dependencies and resident
  expectations.

Phase 1 leaves this overlay as a stub so high-rise properties can load the
suburban- or urban-mid-rise overlay as a fallback while the full high-rise pack is
authored.
