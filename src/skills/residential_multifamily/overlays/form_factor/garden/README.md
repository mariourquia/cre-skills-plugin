# Garden-Style Form Factor

Garden-style properties are typically two to three stories, surface parking, no
elevators, with grounds-intensive common areas. The overlay implies:

- Amenity program: pool plus leasing office plus modest fitness in most markets.
  Amenity density per unit is low compared with mid-rise or urban.
- Common-area maintenance: breezeways, exterior stairs, surface parking striping,
  landscaping, exterior lighting, and irrigation are primary cost drivers.
- Parking mix: open surface parking; covered parking if present is typically a
  small share. Detached garages are sometimes a premium add.
- Unit mix: typically balanced 1BR/2BR with some 3BR in family-oriented submarkets.
- Turn scope: standard scope; no elevator constraints or floor-staging complexity.
  Turn-logistics benefit in garden because large items move through ground-floor
  entries.
- Staffing: higher porter/grounds share of total site labor. No elevator-vendor
  relationship required.

The overlay does not carry numeric cadences. All numeric targets (cadences, ratios,
make-ready bands) are referenced from rows in
`reference/derived/role_kpi_targets.csv` prefixed `row_ff_garden_*`.

## Interactions

- Combine with segment overlay (middle-market default).
- Combine with lifecycle overlay (lease_up, stabilized, renovation, recap_support).
- Combine with management-mode overlay (self_managed or third_party_managed plus
  owner_oversight).
