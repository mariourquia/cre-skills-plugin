# Wrap Form Factor

Wrap properties feature residential units wrapping a structured parking deck. Four
to six stories of residential surround a precast or cast-in-place garage. The
overlay implies:

- Amenity program: pool, fitness, leasing office, sometimes a club room or dog
  park. Amenity density is higher than garden, lower than urban mid-rise.
- Common-area maintenance: includes the structured parking deck (striping, signage,
  bollards, lighting, expansion joints, leakage monitoring). Dog-park cleanup is
  typically present.
- Parking mix: covered structured parking is the default. Garages or reserved
  stalls may carry a premium.
- Pool and elevator: pool typically present. Elevator count is modest (often two
  or three serving the residential floors).
- Unit mix: balanced 1BR / 2BR with modest 3BR share.
- Turn scope: standard; corridor access simplifies move-in compared with walk-up.
- Staffing: closer to suburban mid-rise than garden. Elevator-vendor relationship
  required; structured-parking maintenance adds a vendor.

Numeric cadences and ratios live in reference rows prefixed `row_ff_wrap_*`.
