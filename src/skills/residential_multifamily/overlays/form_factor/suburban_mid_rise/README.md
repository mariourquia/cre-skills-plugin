# Suburban Mid-Rise Form Factor

Suburban mid-rise properties are four to six stories with structured parking on a
single site in a suburban submarket. The overlay implies:

- Amenity program: full program with pool, fitness, club room, package room, often
  pet spa or dog park. Amenity density per unit is higher than wrap.
- Common-area maintenance: amenity cadence is a primary cost driver. Corridors,
  lobby, leasing office, pool deck.
- Parking mix: structured parking with covered and often reserved options.
  Garage and reserved-stall premiums are common.
- Pool and elevator: pool typically present; two to four elevators serving
  residential.
- Unit mix: 1BR-heavy with meaningful 2BR share; modest 3BR.
- Turn scope: standard; elevator logistics add a small scheduling overhead for
  move-ins and turns.
- Staffing: full leasing team, maintenance team, porter, often a part-time or
  full-time concierge depending on segment.

Numeric cadences and ratios live in reference rows prefixed
`row_ff_suburban_mid_rise_*`.
