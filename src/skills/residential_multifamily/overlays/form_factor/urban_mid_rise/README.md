# Urban Mid-Rise Form Factor

Urban mid-rise properties are five to eight stories, often with vertical mixed use
(ground-floor retail, sometimes structured parking below grade). The overlay
implies:

- Amenity program: full plus rooftop / lifestyle amenities (rooftop deck, coworking,
  curated fitness). Amenity density per unit is higher than suburban mid-rise.
- Common-area maintenance: lobby, corridors, elevator cabs, rooftop, trash room,
  package room, bike room. Waste management and package logistics are larger line
  items than in suburban form factors.
- Parking mix: structured parking is common; below-grade structured parking adds
  waterproofing and drainage surfaces to maintenance scope.
- Pool and elevator: pool typically present in markets that support it; elevator
  count scales with height. Elevator maintenance is a material line item.
- Unit mix: heavy studio / 1BR with modest 2BR; 3BR rare.
- Turn scope: standard, plus elevator move-in staging affects scheduling. Vendor
  access requires loading-dock coordination in many buildings.
- Staffing: full leasing team, full maintenance team, porter, often concierge or
  front-desk role. Retail-tenant coordination adds an asset-management surface if
  the building houses ground-floor retail.

Numeric cadences and ratios live in reference rows prefixed
`row_ff_urban_mid_rise_*`.

## Interactions

- Combine with segment, lifecycle, and management mode overlays.
