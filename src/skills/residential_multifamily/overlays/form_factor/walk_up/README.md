# Walk-Up Form Factor

Walk-up properties are small-footprint, no-elevator buildings, often urban infill.
The overlay implies:

- Amenity program: minimal. Laundry room or in-unit laundry; sometimes a small
  shared roof deck or backyard. Pool and fitness are uncommon.
- Common-area maintenance: stairwells, entry vestibules, and exterior steps are the
  primary surfaces. Landscaping is limited.
- Parking mix: street parking or no parking is common in urban walk-ups. Where
  parking exists, it is typically surface or small surface lot.
- Pool and elevator: none. No elevator-vendor relationship required.
- Unit mix: heavy 1BR and studio in urban submarkets; small 2BR share.
- Turn scope: standard, but upper-floor walk-ups add labor time for heavy items
  (appliances, flooring rolls) and affect turn-labor budget.
- Staffing: leanest of the form factors. No porter dedicated to amenities;
  maintenance is generalist.

Numeric cadences and ratios live in reference rows prefixed `row_ff_walk_up_*`.
