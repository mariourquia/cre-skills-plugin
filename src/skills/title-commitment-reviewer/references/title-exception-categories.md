# Title Exception Categories

Reference for SKILL.md Workflow 3 (Schedule B-II Exception Review). Use this matrix to categorize every B-II exception and assign a treatment. The Tier column maps directly to the three-tier acceptability framework in the skill.

---

## Standard Exceptions by State Type

### Title Company States (majority of US)

Title companies search their own title plants and issue commitments directly. Standard exceptions are narrower and more predictable.

**Typical standard exceptions included in all commitments:**

| Exception | Description | Normally Tier |
|---|---|---|
| Taxes not yet due and payable | Current year and future taxes | 1 |
| Survey matters | Discrepancies, conflicts, encroachments that a survey would show | 2 (deletable with ALTA survey) |
| Rights of parties in possession | Tenant or occupant claims not shown by public record | 2 (deletable with estoppels) |
| Easements or claims of easements not shown by public record | Prescriptive easements, implied easements | 2-3 |
| Mechanics liens, laborers liens | Claims for labor/materials not yet filed | 2 (deletable with contractor affidavits) |
| Water rights, claims, or title | If applicable in western states | 2-3 (state-specific) |
| Mineral rights and subsurface interests | If mineral rights were severed | 2-3 (ALTA 35 endorsement) |

### Attorney States (NY, MA, SC, CT, DE, GA, WV)

Title is based on attorney's opinion. Standard exceptions are broader because attorney's examination scope varies. The title company's commitment incorporates the attorney's exceptions by reference.

**Additional standard exceptions common in attorney states:**

| Exception | Description | Treatment |
|---|---|---|
| Attorney's general exception | "Such matters as an accurate survey would show" (broader than standard survey exception) | Deletable with ALTA survey + no-survey exception waiver |
| Covenants and restrictions of record | All deed restrictions and covenants in chain | Review each; tier individually |
| Rights of tenants in possession | Tenant rights under leases not recorded | Estoppels + SNDA; ALTA 17 |
| Easements of record | All easements in chain | Map each to survey; assess encroachment |
| State and local assessment liens | Improvement district assessments | Confirm paid; lien release |

### California (CLTA Policy Standard)

California uses the California Land Title Association (CLTA) form, which is not identical to ALTA. Key differences:

- Standard exceptions in CLTA are broader than ALTA
- "Preliminary report" is the California title document (same function as commitment)
- Survey exception is rarely deleted (ALTA survey less common in CA; CLTA survey exception broader)
- Mechanic's lien: California has a 90-day period; preliminary notice must be given to owner
- Mello-Roos and CFD: statutory super-priority; always in B-II; confirm current balance
- Tax reassessment (Prop 19): note that transfer is a reassessment trigger; not a title defect but a tax consequence

### Texas (Promulgated Forms)

Texas uses Texas Department of Insurance (TDI) promulgated forms (T-1 through T-51). Exceptions are more limited than ALTA because promulgated forms specify what can and cannot be excepted.

- Survey exception: Texas Title Insurance Manual, Section P-2; can be deleted with Texas survey
- Mechanic's lien: Texas Constitution Article XVI, Section 37 gives constitutional lien rights; no advance waiver permitted; joint check agreement is the only real protection
- Standard exception for "visible and apparent easements": reviewable against survey
- No transfer tax; recording fees only

## B-II Exception Acceptability Matrix

### Category 1: Tax and Assessment Exceptions

| Exception | Tier | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| General taxes, current year, not yet due | 1 -- Accept | Standard; pro-rate at closing |
| General taxes, prior years, paid | 1 -- Accept | Confirm receipts on file |
| General taxes, prior years, delinquent | 3 -- Unacceptable | Payoff required; calculate penalties |
| Special assessments, current and future | 1-2 | Confirm current assessment balance; flag if installments remain |
| Mello-Roos / CFD (California) | 2 -- Negotiate | Confirm balance and annual payment; disclose to buyer |
| HOA assessments (FL, NV super-priority) | 2 -- Negotiate | HOA estoppel required; confirm no delinquency |
| BID / improvement district | 1-2 | Confirm current; note annual cost in pro forma |

### Category 2: Survey and Physical Matters

| Exception | Tier | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Survey matters, general | 2 -- Negotiate | Deletable with ALTA/NSPS survey and no-survey exception waiver request |
| Easement, utility (not encroaching) | 1 -- Accept | Map on survey; confirm no building under or within easement |
| Easement, utility (under building footprint) | 3 -- Unacceptable | Relocation required or ALTA 9 + utility company consent |
| Easement, drainage (non-encroaching) | 1 -- Accept | Confirm not in building or parking field |
| Easement, drainage (encroaching) | 2-3 | Assess impact; may require drainage authority consent |
| Easement, access / ingress-egress (appurtenant) | 1 -- Accept | Confirm easement is appurtenant and sufficient for use |
| Easement, access / ingress-egress (in gross) | 2 -- Negotiate | In gross easements do not run with land; confirm terms |
| Encroachment, fence or minor structure | 2 -- Negotiate | Survey exception + affirmative coverage; or boundary line agreement |
| Encroachment, building footprint | 3 -- Unacceptable | Must resolve; boundary line agreement or quiet title |
| Setback violation (improvement in setback) | 2 -- Negotiate | ALTA 3.1 for zoning setback; deed restriction setback is harder |
| Flood zone designation | 1-2 | Note zone; Zone AE/VE requires flood insurance; consider ALTA 34 |
| Mineral rights severed | 2-3 | Assess surface use rights; ALTA 35 endorsement; oil/gas leases are critical |

### Category 3: Deed Restrictions and Covenants (CC&Rs)

| Exception | Tier | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Use restriction consistent with intended use | 1 -- Accept | Confirm intended use is permitted; document in file |
| Use restriction conflicting with intended use | 3 -- Unacceptable | Deal-breaker unless restriction can be released or waived |
| Use restriction (ambiguous) | 2 -- Negotiate | Obtain local counsel opinion; may be unenforceable if unreasonable in duration |
| Architectural review requirement | 2 -- Negotiate | Confirm who holds approval right; delay risk for redevelopment |
| Right of first refusal held by HOA or third party | 2-3 | ROFR notice may be required; waiver needed before closing |
| Reverter or condition subsequent | 3 -- Unacceptable | If condition has not been met, deed may be void; investigate |
| Plat dedication (streets, parks, utilities) | 1 -- Accept | Standard; confirm dedication does not impair parking or access |

### Category 4: Recorded Instruments and Claims

| Exception | Tier | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Prior deed of trust / mortgage (being paid off) | 1-2 | B-I payoff requirement; release to record at closing |
| Prior deed of trust / mortgage (not being paid off) | 3 -- Unacceptable | Must be released or assumed; lender will not insure behind |
| Judgment lien (being paid off) | 1-2 | Confirm judgment amount including interest; require recorded release |
| Judgment lien (not being paid off) | 3 -- Unacceptable | Lien primes buyer's title; must be paid or bond off |
| Mechanic's lien (filed, within lien period) | 3 -- Unacceptable | Pay, bond off, or obtain release before closing |
| Mechanic's lien (filed, outside lien period but unresolved) | 2-3 | Older liens may be expired; confirm with local counsel |
| UCC fixture filing (active) | 2 -- Negotiate | Termination statement required from secured party |
| IRS / federal tax lien | 3 -- Unacceptable | IRS Certificate of Discharge required; 30+ days |
| Lis pendens (active litigation) | 3 -- Unacceptable | Deal-breaker until litigation resolved or bonded off |
| Environmental lien (CERCLA) | 3 -- Unacceptable | ALTA 8.1 provides limited coverage; investigate EPA records |
| Ground lease | 2-3 | Review ground lease terms; leasehold policy required if buying leasehold |

### Category 5: Rights of Parties in Possession

| Exception | Tier | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Rights of parties in possession (no tenants) | 1-2 | Seller's affidavit of no unrecorded leases or occupants |
| Rights of parties in possession (known tenants, estoppels received) | 2 -- Negotiate | Deletable with full estoppels + SNDAs; ALTA 17 confirms access |
| Rights of parties in possession (tenants, no estoppels) | 2-3 | Cannot close without estoppels from major tenants; lender will require |
| Month-to-month tenancy | 2 -- Negotiate | Note termination rights; confirm in estoppel |
| Holdover tenant | 3 | Eviction or negotiated exit before closing; creates possession risk |

## Endorsement Premium Reference (Approximate)

Premiums are percentage of the policy premium or flat fees. Actual pricing is underwriter-specific and market-specific. Use as a budget estimate only; confirm with title officer.

| Endorsement | Typical Premium | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| ALTA 3.1 (Zoning) | 10-15% of base premium | Requires zoning compliance letter |
| ALTA 8.1 (Environmental protection lien) | 5-10% of base premium | Standard for commercial |
| ALTA 9 (Restrictions, encroachments, minerals) | 10-20% of base premium | Requires ALTA/NSPS survey |
| ALTA 14 (Future advance) | 5-10% of base premium | For revolving credit facilities |
| ALTA 15 (Nonimputation) | 5% of base premium | Common in acquisitions with co-investors |
| ALTA 16 (Date-down / gap) | $500-$2,500 flat | Scales with gap period length |
| ALTA 17 (Access and entry) | 5% of base premium | Standard commercial |
| ALTA 17.2 (Utility access) | 5% of base premium | If utility connections not on survey |
| ALTA 18 (Single tax parcel) | $250-$1,000 flat | Per parcel confirmation |
| ALTA 19 (Contiguity) | $500-$2,500 flat | Per assemblage; scales with parcels |
| ALTA 22 (Location) | 5% of base premium | Confirms improvements on insured land |
| ALTA 25 (Same as survey) | 5% of base premium | Ties survey depiction to insured description |
| ALTA 28 (Easement damage) | 5-10% of base premium | For encroachments into easements |
| ALTA 35 (Minerals) | 10-15% of base premium | If mineral rights are severed |
| ALTA 36 (Energy project) | Negotiated | Wind, solar, energy-specific coverage |
