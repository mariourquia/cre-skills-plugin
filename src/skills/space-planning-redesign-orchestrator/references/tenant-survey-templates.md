# Tenant Survey Templates

Ready-to-deploy survey instruments for property managers evaluating space redesigns and amenity additions. Three instruments cover the full lifecycle: demand discovery, concept validation, and post-renovation feedback.

---

## Survey 1: General Amenity Demand Survey

**Purpose**: Discover which amenities tenants value most, gauge willingness to pay, and assess satisfaction with current common areas.
**When to use**: Before any design work begins. Baseline demand assessment.
**Target audience**: All tenants/residents in the property.

### Questions

**Q1. How satisfied are you with the current common area amenities at this property?**
- Very satisfied (5)
- Satisfied (4)
- Neutral (3)
- Dissatisfied (2)
- Very dissatisfied (1)

*Scoring: Mean score. Below 3.0 = strong signal for investment.*

**Q2. Which of the following amenities would you most like to see added or improved? (Select up to 5)**
- [ ] Fitness center / gym
- [ ] Coworking / shared workspace
- [ ] Lobby upgrade
- [ ] Conference / meeting rooms
- [ ] Tenant lounge / coffee bar
- [ ] Outdoor terrace or patio
- [ ] Package lockers
- [ ] Pet area / dog park
- [ ] Bike storage and repair
- [ ] EV charging stations
- [ ] Rooftop deck
- [ ] Childcare facility
- [ ] Food service / cafe
- [ ] Game room / recreation
- [ ] Meditation / wellness room
- [ ] Podcast / content studio
- [ ] Other: ___________

*Scoring: Rank by selection frequency. Top 3-5 selections form the shortlist.*

**Q3. How often would you use a shared workspace or coworking area if one were available on-site?**
- Daily (5)
- 3-4 times per week (4)
- 1-2 times per week (3)
- A few times per month (2)
- Rarely or never (1)

*Scoring: Mean score. Above 3.0 = strong coworking demand signal.*

**Q4. How often would you use an on-site fitness center if one were available?**
- Daily (5)
- 3-4 times per week (4)
- 1-2 times per week (3)
- A few times per month (2)
- Rarely or never (1)

*Scoring: Mean score. Above 3.0 = strong fitness demand signal.*

**Q5. What times of day would you most likely use common area amenities? (Select all that apply)**
- [ ] Early morning (before 8 AM)
- [ ] Morning (8 AM - 12 PM)
- [ ] Lunch (12 PM - 2 PM)
- [ ] Afternoon (2 PM - 5 PM)
- [ ] Evening (5 PM - 8 PM)
- [ ] Night (after 8 PM)
- [ ] Weekends

*Scoring: Frequency distribution. Informs operating hours and staffing.*

**Q6. Would you be willing to pay a modest increase in rent for significantly improved amenities?**
- Yes, up to 5% more (3)
- Yes, up to 2-3% more (2)
- Only if included at current rent (1)
- No, I would not value additional amenities (0)

*Scoring: Weighted mean. Score of 1.5+ = rent premium is achievable.*

**Q7. How important are building amenities in your decision to renew your lease?**
- Very important -- a primary factor (5)
- Important -- one of several factors (4)
- Somewhat important (3)
- Not very important (2)
- Not a factor at all (1)

*Scoring: Mean score. Above 3.5 = amenities are a retention lever.*

**Q8. Have you considered moving to another property because of better amenities?**
- Yes, I am actively looking (4)
- Yes, I have considered it (3)
- No, but I have noticed competitors with better amenities (2)
- No, amenities are not a factor (1)

*Scoring: Score of 3+ from >20% of respondents = urgent competitive threat.*

**Q9. How would you rate the current lobby and building entrance?**
- Excellent -- modern and welcoming (5)
- Good -- adequate but could be updated (4)
- Fair -- feels dated (3)
- Poor -- needs significant improvement (2)
- Very poor -- embarrassing to visitors (1)

*Scoring: Mean score. Below 3.0 = lobby renovation is a priority.*

**Q10. How would you rate the building's outdoor spaces (if any)?**
- Excellent (5)
- Good (4)
- Fair (3)
- Poor (2)
- No outdoor spaces available (N/A)

*Scoring: Mean score excluding N/A. High N/A count = opportunity to create outdoor space.*

**Q11. What is the single biggest improvement the property could make to common areas?**
- [Open text response]

*Scoring: Qualitative. Code responses into themes. Top 3 themes inform design brief.*

**Q12. Is there anything else you would like to share about your experience with the building's amenities and common areas?**
- [Open text response]

*Scoring: Qualitative. Look for recurring complaints, specific requests, and competitive mentions.*

## Survey 2: Specific Amenity Validation Survey

**Purpose**: Test demand for a specific proposed amenity before committing capital. Validate the concept, gauge usage commitment, and surface design preferences.
**When to use**: After the general demand survey identifies a leading amenity concept, or when ownership has a specific idea to validate.
**Target audience**: All tenants/residents. Customize the amenity name in brackets below.

### Questions

**Q1. We are considering adding a [specific amenity] to the building. How interested would you be in this amenity?**
- Extremely interested -- I would use it regularly (5)
- Very interested -- I would use it occasionally (4)
- Somewhat interested -- I might try it (3)
- Not very interested (2)
- Not interested at all (1)

*Scoring: Mean score. Above 3.5 = proceed with design. 2.5-3.5 = refine concept. Below 2.5 = reconsider.*

**Q2. If this [specific amenity] were available, how often would you realistically use it?**
- Daily (5)
- 3-4 times per week (4)
- 1-2 times per week (3)
- A few times per month (2)
- Rarely or never (1)

*Scoring: Mean score. Multiply by respondent count for utilization projection.*

**Q3. Which of these features are most important to you in a [specific amenity]? (Rank your top 3)**
- [ ] Size and spaciousness
- [ ] Quality of equipment/furnishings
- [ ] Modern design and aesthetics
- [ ] Extended hours of availability
- [ ] Natural light
- [ ] Privacy / low noise
- [ ] Technology integration
- [ ] Proximity to your unit/suite
- [ ] Professional staffing/management

*Scoring: Weighted rank (1st = 3 pts, 2nd = 2 pts, 3rd = 1 pt). Top features guide design spec.*

**Q4. Which design style do you prefer for common area spaces?**
- Modern / minimalist (clean lines, neutral palette)
- Warm / hospitality (wood, soft textures, residential feel)
- Industrial / creative (exposed materials, bold colors)
- Biophilic / natural (plants, natural materials, earth tones)
- No preference

*Scoring: Plurality wins. Informs design visualizer aesthetic direction.*

**Q5. Would you be willing to pay a small monthly fee for access to this [specific amenity]?**
- Yes, up to $[high tier] per month (4)
- Yes, up to $[mid tier] per month (3)
- Only if included in my rent/CAM (2)
- I would not use this amenity regardless of cost (1)

*Scoring: Revenue projection = (respondents at each tier x fee) x 12. Fee tiers should be set based on amenity type and market.*

**Q6. If this [specific amenity] were under construction, how much temporary disruption would you tolerate?**
- Up to 6 months of construction is fine (4)
- Up to 3 months is acceptable (3)
- Only if disruption is minimal (1-2 months) (2)
- Any disruption is unacceptable (1)

*Scoring: Mean score. Below 2.0 = plan construction during low-occupancy period or phased approach.*

**Q7. Would the addition of this [specific amenity] make you more likely to renew your lease?**
- Significantly more likely (4)
- Somewhat more likely (3)
- No impact on my decision (2)
- I am not planning to renew regardless (1)

*Scoring: Score of 3+ from >40% of respondents = strong retention justification.*

**Q8. What would make this [specific amenity] most valuable to you? (Open response)**
- [Open text response]

*Scoring: Qualitative. Code into design requirements. Feed directly to design visualizer subagent.*

## Survey 3: Post-Renovation Satisfaction Survey

**Purpose**: Measure success of the completed amenity investment. Assess usage, satisfaction, and impact on retention and referrals.
**When to use**: 60-90 days after the amenity opens. Repeat at 6 months and 12 months.
**Target audience**: All tenants/residents.

### Questions

**Q1. How often do you use the new [specific amenity]?**
- Daily (5)
- 3-4 times per week (4)
- 1-2 times per week (3)
- A few times per month (2)
- I have not used it (1)

*Scoring: Mean score. Compare to pre-renovation projected usage (Survey 2, Q2).*

**Q2. How satisfied are you with the new [specific amenity]?**
- Very satisfied (5)
- Satisfied (4)
- Neutral (3)
- Dissatisfied (2)
- Very dissatisfied (1)

*Scoring: Mean score. Target 4.0+. Below 3.0 = investigate and address issues.*

**Q3. How does the new [specific amenity] compare to similar amenities at other properties you have experienced?**
- Best I have seen (5)
- Better than most (4)
- About average (3)
- Below average (2)
- Worst I have seen (1)

*Scoring: Mean score. Benchmark against competitive set. Target 4.0+ for competitive advantage.*

**Q4. What do you like most about the new [specific amenity]?**
- [Open text response]

*Scoring: Qualitative. Identify features to highlight in marketing and leasing materials.*

**Q5. What would you improve about the new [specific amenity]?**
- [Open text response]

*Scoring: Qualitative. Code into actionable improvements. Quick wins vs. future phases.*

**Q6. Has the new [specific amenity] made you more likely to renew your lease?**
- Significantly more likely (4)
- Somewhat more likely (3)
- No impact (2)
- Less likely (renovation disruption was negative) (1)

*Scoring: Compare to pre-renovation Survey 2 Q7. Delta = measured retention impact.*

**Q7. How likely are you to recommend this property to a friend or colleague because of its amenities?**
- 0-10 scale (NPS format)

*Scoring: NPS = % Promoters (9-10) minus % Detractors (0-6). Track NPS over time.*

**Q8. Overall, how would you rate the value of the building's amenities relative to your rent?**
- Excellent value (5)
- Good value (4)
- Fair value (3)
- Poor value (2)
- Very poor value (1)

*Scoring: Mean score. Above 3.5 = amenity investment is perceived positively by tenants.*

## Survey Design Notes

### Distribution Methods and Expected Response Rates

| Method | Expected Response Rate | Best For | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Email (property management system) | 15-25% | All property types | Send 2 reminders at 3-day intervals |
| Property app push notification | 20-35% | Multifamily with app | Highest engagement for residents |
| QR code in lobby/elevator | 5-10% | Office, mixed-use | Passive collection, low effort to deploy |
| Door flyer with QR code | 10-15% | Multifamily | Good for properties without tenant email |
| In-person during events | 40-60% | All types | Highest rate but potential selection bias |
| Tablet kiosk in lobby | 8-15% | Office | Captures visitors and tenants |

### Sample Size Guidance

| Building Population | Minimum Responses | Target Responses | Confidence / Margin |
|---|---|---|---|
| < 50 tenants/units | 25 | 35+ | 90% confidence, +/- 10% margin |
| 50-100 | 40 | 60+ | 90% confidence, +/- 8% margin |
| 100-250 | 60 | 100+ | 95% confidence, +/- 8% margin |
| 250-500 | 80 | 150+ | 95% confidence, +/- 6% margin |
| 500+ | 100 | 200+ | 95% confidence, +/- 5% margin |

### Statistical Notes

- **Minimum viable sample**: 30 responses for any quantitative analysis. Below 30, treat results as directional only.
- **Significance testing**: For comparing two groups (e.g., office vs. retail tenants), use a two-sample t-test. Require p < 0.05.
- **Non-response bias**: If response rate is below 15%, results may not represent the full tenant population. Supplement with informal interviews.
- **Weighting**: For mixed-use properties, weight responses by SF occupied to prevent small tenants from dominating results.
- **Longitudinal tracking**: Use the same questions across survey waves to track trends. Changing question wording breaks comparability.
