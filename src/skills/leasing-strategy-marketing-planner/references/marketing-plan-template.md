# Annual Leasing Marketing Plan Template

## Purpose

Structured template for developing annual leasing marketing plans for institutional CRE assets. Includes target tenant profiling, channel strategy, budget allocation, seasonal calendar, and KPI tracking.

#### Channel Details

**1. Digital Listings (CoStar, LoopNet, CREXi, Crexi)**

- Premium listing on CoStar and LoopNet for all availabilities > 2,000 SF
- Professional photography (minimum 15 images per listing)
- Floor plans (dimensioned, furnished layout options)
- 3D virtual tour (Matterport or equivalent)
- Listing copy: benefit-led headline, 200-300 word description, full specs
- Refresh cycle: update photos quarterly, update pricing monthly, respond to inquiries within 2 hours

**2. Broker Co-Op Program**

- Quarterly broker open house or market update event
- Monthly email blast to broker database (200+ contacts)
- Cooperative commission at or above market rate
- Broker incentive: $X,XXX bonus for first lease signed in Q1 (if applicable)
- Annual broker appreciation event (holiday party, golf outing)
- Top broker recognition: quarterly acknowledgment, annual award

**3. Direct Outreach**

- Monthly broker visits to top 20 tenant rep firms in market
- Quarterly lunch with top 5 producing brokers
- Direct mail to target tenant prospects (identified via data services)
- LinkedIn outreach to decision-makers at target companies
- Cold calling to identified prospects (in-house or outsourced)

**4. Digital Advertising**

- LinkedIn Sponsored Content targeting decision-makers in target industries
- Google Ads for relevant search terms ("[submarket] office space", "[city] commercial lease")
- Retargeting ads for website visitors
- Email campaigns to curated prospect lists (purchased or built)

**5. Signage and Branding**

- Building-mounted banner or sign (check municipal signage ordinances)
- Monument/pylon sign (if ground-level access)
- Interior lobby signage for available suites
- Sidewalk A-frame signs (if permitted)
- Vehicle wrap or parking garage signage

#### Seasonal Adjustments

- **Q1-Q2**: Heaviest marketing spend. Most tenants begin their search 9-12 months before lease expiration. Spring is peak touring season.
- **Q3**: Moderate spend. Late summer can be slow. Use the time for content refresh and broker relationship building. Many large requirements surface in August-September.
- **Q4**: Moderate spend with urgency messaging. "Secure your space before year-end." Holiday events for tenant retention. Begin next year planning.

### Section 6: Reporting

#### Weekly Report (During Active Lease-Up)

- New inquiries received (count, source)
- Tours scheduled and conducted

- Competitive intelligence (new availabilities, pricing changes)

#### Monthly Report

- Full funnel metrics vs targets

- Year-to-date performance vs annual plan

- Budget reallocation requests (if any)

## Worked Example: 150K SF Suburban Office Marketing Plan

### Scenario
Meridian Business Center, a 150,000 SF Class A suburban office building in a Southeast suburban market. 30,000 SF (20%) vacant across 3 suites. Submarket vacancy is 15%. Asking rent $28/SF NNN.

### Annual Budget: $85,000

| Channel | Budget | Activity |
|---------|--------|----------|
| CoStar/LoopNet Premium | $18,000 | Premium listings for all 3 suites |
| Broker Events (2x) | $12,000 | Spring open house, Fall appreciation lunch |
| Direct Outreach | $8,000 | Monthly broker visits, prospect mailing |
| Digital Ads (LinkedIn, Google) | $15,000 | Targeted campaigns Q1-Q3 |
| Photography/Virtual Tour | $8,000 | Professional shoot + Matterport |
| Signage | $6,000 | Building banner, monument sign update |
| Print Collateral | $5,000 | Flyers, brochures for tours |
| Spec Suite (1x 5,000 SF) | $10,000 | Light cosmetic refresh, staging |
| Contingency | $3,000 | |
| **Total** | **$85,000** | |

### Annual Targets
- Inquiries: 120 (10/month)
- Tours: 36 (3/month)
- Proposals: 12
- Leases signed: 4-5 (targeting 25-30K SF absorbed)
- Marketing cost per SF leased: $2.83-3.40/SF
