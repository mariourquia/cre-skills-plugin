# Deal Sourcing Outreach Template Library

## Direct Mail Sequence (3-Letter Campaign)

### Letter 1: Introduction (Week 0)

```
[Company Letterhead]
[Date]

[Owner Name]
[Owner Address]

RE: [Property Address] -- Expression of Interest

Dear [Mr./Ms. Last Name],

I am writing to express our firm's interest in acquiring [Property Address],
your [property type] property in [submarket/neighborhood].

[Company Name] is an active [property type] investor in the [market name]
metro area. We have acquired [X] properties totaling [X] units/SF in the
past [X] months and are seeking to expand our portfolio in [submarket].

We are prepared to offer:
- Competitive pricing based on current market conditions
- Flexible closing timeline (30-90 days)
- All-cash or conventional financing (your preference)
- As-is condition (no inspection contingencies beyond standard diligence)

If you have any interest in exploring a sale, I would welcome a brief
conversation at your convenience. There is no obligation, and our
discussion would remain confidential.

Sincerely,

[Name]
[Title]
[Company]
[Phone]
[Email]
```

### Letter 2: Follow-Up with Value Proposition (Week 3)

```
[Company Letterhead]
[Date]

[Owner Name]
[Owner Address]

RE: Follow-Up -- [Property Address]

Dear [Mr./Ms. Last Name],

I wrote to you a few weeks ago regarding your property at [Property Address].
I understand you may not be actively marketing the property, which is
precisely why we are reaching out directly.

Since my last letter, we have closed on [recent comparable transaction --
address, price, or description] in [nearby submarket], and our appetite
for additional acquisitions in [target submarket] remains strong.

A few reasons owners in your position choose to work with us:

1. CERTAINTY OF CLOSE: We have [$ amount] in committed equity and
   [pre-approved/relationship] financing. We close on time.

2. CONFIDENTIALITY: Off-market transactions avoid the disruption of
   a public listing -- no sign in the yard, no broker calls, no tire-kickers.

3. SIMPLICITY: We handle diligence efficiently and minimize your
   time commitment. One conversation is all it takes to get started.

[MARKET-SPECIFIC INSERT: e.g., "With interest rates stabilizing and
cap rates compressing in [submarket], the current window may represent
an attractive exit point for your investment."]

I will follow up by phone in the coming days. In the meantime, feel
free to reach me directly at [phone] or [email].

Respectfully,

[Name]
[Title]
```

### Letter 3: Final Touch / Soft Close (Week 7)

```
[Company Letterhead]
[Date]

[Owner Name]
[Owner Address]

RE: Final Note -- [Property Address]

Dear [Mr./Ms. Last Name],

This is my final correspondence regarding [Property Address]. I respect
your time and do not want to be a nuisance.

I will keep your property in our tracking system. If circumstances change --
whether in six months or six years -- I hope you will consider reaching
out. Our interest is genuine and our offer to have a confidential
conversation remains open.

For your records, here is my contact information:

  [Name] | [Title]
  [Company Name]
  [Phone] | [Email]
  [Website]

Wishing you continued success with the property.

Best regards,

[Name]
```

## Email Cold Sequence (3-Email Campaign)

### Email 1: Initial Outreach

**Subject line options (A/B test):**
- "Quick question about [Property Address]"
- "[Owner First Name] -- interest in [Property Address]"
- "Off-market offer for your [property type] in [submarket]"

```
Hi [First Name],

I'm [Your Name] with [Company]. We're active [property type] buyers
in [market/submarket] and have been tracking [Property Address].

Would you be open to a brief call to discuss whether a sale might
make sense for you? No pressure -- just a conversation.

We can typically provide a preliminary offer within 48 hours of
receiving basic property information.

Best,
[Name]
[Title] | [Company]
[Phone]
```

### Email 2: Value Add / Social Proof (Day 4)

**Subject line:** "Re: Quick question about [Property Address]"

```
Hi [First Name],

Following up on my note from earlier this week. I wanted to share
some context on why we're interested:

- We recently acquired [comparable property] in [nearby area] for
  [$X.XM / $X/unit / $X/SF]
- We're looking to add [X] more [property type] properties in
  [submarket] this year
- We have [$ amount] in equity committed and ready to deploy

If now isn't the right time, I completely understand. But if you've
ever thought about selling -- or are curious what the property might
be worth today -- I'd be happy to share our perspective.

Worth a 10-minute call?

[Name]
[Phone]
```

### Email 3: Breakup Email (Day 10)

**Subject line:** "Closing the loop -- [Property Address]"

```
Hi [First Name],

I've reached out a couple of times and haven't heard back, so I'll
assume the timing isn't right. No worries at all.

I'll keep your property on our watch list. If things change on your
end -- change in partnership, upcoming capital needs, retirement
planning, 1031 deadline -- please don't hesitate to reach out.

My door is always open: [phone] | [email]

Best,
[Name]
```

## Cold Call Script

### Opening

```
"Hi, is this [Owner Name]? ... Great, this is [Your Name] with
[Company Name]. I'm calling about your property at [Address].
Do you have 60 seconds?"

[If yes, continue. If no:]
"No problem. When would be a better time for a quick call?"
[Schedule callback, confirm number]
```

### Value Proposition (30 seconds)

```
"We're an active buyer of [property type] in [market]. We've been
tracking your property and wanted to see if you'd have any interest
in discussing a potential sale. We buy off-market, close quickly,
and keep everything confidential."
```

### Qualifying Questions

```
1. "Have you given any thought to selling in the next 12-24 months?"
2. "How long have you owned the property?"
3. "Is the property performing the way you'd like it to?"
4. "Are there any capital needs coming up -- roof, HVAC, code compliance?"
5. "Do you own other properties in the area?"
6. "What would need to be true for you to consider a sale?"
```

### Objection Handlers

**1. "I'm not interested in selling."**
```
"I understand completely. Most of the deals we close start with
owners who weren't actively looking to sell. Mind if I ask -- is
there a price at which you would consider it? ... I'm not trying
to lowball you. I just want to understand if there's a number
that would make it worth your time."
```

**2. "What are you offering?"**
```
"Great question. I don't want to throw out a number without
understanding the property better. Can I ask a few quick questions
about occupancy and income? That way I can come back with something
that's actually meaningful. ... [Ask: current occupancy, approximate
NOI or gross rent, any deferred maintenance, lease terms]"
```

**3. "I need to talk to my partner/spouse/attorney."**
```
"Of course. That makes total sense. Would it be helpful if I sent
over a brief written summary of who we are and what we're proposing?
That way they have something to review. What's the best email for
that? ... And when should I follow up -- end of this week or early
next week?"
```

**4. "I already have a broker."**
```
"Understood. Is the property currently listed? ... [If yes:] Got it.
Would you mind sharing the listing details? We're happy to work with
your broker. [If no:] We're happy to work alongside your broker or
directly with you -- whatever you prefer. Our goal is just to see
if there's a deal that works for both sides."
```

**5. "I just bought it / I'm not ready yet."**
```
"Makes sense. How long have you owned it? ... And what's your
general hold strategy -- are you thinking 3-5 years, longer? ...
Great. Mind if I check back in [appropriate timeframe]? I'll make
a note so I don't bother you before then."
```

**6. "Your offer will be too low."**
```
"I hear you, and I don't want to waste your time. Here's what I
can tell you: we've been paying [recent comp range] for similar
properties in [submarket]. We're not bottom-feeders -- we pay
fair market value because we're buying for long-term hold. Would
you be open to sharing some basic financials so I can come back
with a real number?"
```

**7. "Send me something in writing."**
```
"Absolutely. I'll send you a one-page overview of our firm and
a preliminary indication of interest. What's the best email? ...
And what's the best time to follow up after you've had a chance
to review it -- would [day] work?"
```

**8. "How did you get my information?"**
```
"We use public records and property databases to identify
properties that fit our acquisition criteria. Your property
stood out because [specific reason: location, size, property
type, recent activity]. Everything we do is above board --
we're just trying to connect with owners directly rather than
waiting for properties to hit the market."
```

### Closing

```
[If interested:]
"Great. Here's what I'd suggest as a next step: I'll send you
a brief email confirming our conversation and a couple of questions
about the property. Once I have that info, I can have a preliminary
offer to you within 48 hours. Does that work?"

[If not interested now:]
"I appreciate your time, [Name]. I'll keep your property on our
radar. If anything changes -- change in plans, capital event,
partnership shift -- please don't hesitate to call me directly.
I'll send you my contact info by email. Have a great day."
```

## LinkedIn DM Sequence (3-Message Campaign)

### Message 1: Connection Request Note

```
Hi [First Name] -- I see you own/manage [property type] in [market].
We're active buyers in the area and I'd love to connect. No pitch,
just building relationships with local owners. -- [Your Name]
```

### Message 2: Value-First Follow-Up (Day 3-5 after connection)

```
Thanks for connecting, [First Name]. I came across some interesting
data on [submarket] [property type] cap rates / rent trends / recent
sales. Happy to share if useful.

By the way, we recently closed on [brief comparable]. Always looking
for more opportunities in [market]. If you ever want to talk shop or
explore what your property might be worth in today's market, I'm here.
```

### Message 3: Soft Ask (Day 10-14)

```
[First Name] -- circling back briefly. We have active capital to
deploy in [market] for [property type] and your property at [Address]
fits our buy box well.

Would you be open to a quick call? Even if selling isn't on your radar,
I'd value your perspective on the local market. [Phone] or just reply
here.
```

## Customization Guide by Property Type

| Element | Multifamily | Office | Industrial | Retail |
|---------|-------------|--------|-----------|--------|
| Key metric to cite | Price/unit, cap rate | Price/SF, lease rate | Price/SF, clear height | Price/SF, NOI/SF |
| Seller motivation trigger | Deferred capex, rent control | Vacancy, lease rollover | Below-market rents | Tenant credit risk |
| Value proposition | "We renovate and manage in-house" | "We re-lease and reposition" | "We have tenant demand from logistics" | "We're experienced in mixed-use" |
| Urgency driver | "Rents are peaking, good time to exit" | "Flight to quality favoring new deals" | "Industrial cap rates at historic lows" | "Retail recovery creating exit window" |
| Social proof | "X units acquired in market" | "X SF repositioned successfully" | "X warehouses under management" | "X retail centers in portfolio" |
