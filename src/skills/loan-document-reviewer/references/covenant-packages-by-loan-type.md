# Covenant Packages by Loan Type

Standard covenant packages for each major CRE loan type. Use as the baseline against which to compare the actual loan documents. Deviations that are more restrictive than the standard package are flagged in the loan-document-reviewer skill as negotiation points.

---

## Covenant Package 1: Agency (Freddie Mac / Fannie Mae)

Agency covenants are substantially standardized through the lender's form documents. Deviations from the standard form are either (a) lender additions (flag and negotiate) or (b) program-specific requirements (non-negotiable).

### Financial Covenants

| Covenant | Standard Threshold | Testing Frequency | Cure Period | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| DSCR | 1.25x (market rate); 1.15x (affordable); 1.20x (green) | Annual (at loan anniversary) | 90 days | Lender uses trailing 12-month NCF from rent roll and operating statements |
| LTV | 75% (standard); 80% (affordable/green) | At material event only | N/A (tested at origination; not ongoing) | Annual appraisal not required on standard agency |
| Occupancy | 85% physical (multifamily standard) | Quarterly | 90 days | Cure: lease-up or rent concessions |
| Debt Service Reserve | 2-3 months PITI | Monthly balance verification | 30 days to replenish | Automatically funded from escrow; deficiency triggers replenishment obligation |

### Operational Covenants

| Covenant | Standard Threshold | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Management company change | Lender approval required | Affiliate managers permitted if fee at market; management agreement must be subordinated |
| Lease approval (commercial units only) | All commercial leases require approval | Residential leases do not require approval on multifamily agency loans |
| CapEx approval | > $10,000/item or $50,000 aggregate per year | Routine maintenance below threshold is permitted without approval |
| Affiliate transactions | Market rate required; documentation required | Arms-length standard applies |
| Insurance | Replacement value; lender is additional insured | Lender places force-placed insurance if borrower lapses; charged to borrower |
| Permitted debt | No additional liens on property | Trade payables in ordinary course only |

### Reporting Covenants

| Report | Deadline | Format |
|---|---|---|
| Annual operating statement | 90 days after fiscal year end | P&L by property; certified by borrower |
| Annual rent roll | 90 days after fiscal year end | All units with rent, occupancy, lease term |
| Quarterly occupancy report | 30 days after quarter end | Physical and economic occupancy |
| Borrower financial statements | 90 days after fiscal year end | Audited for larger portfolios; unaudited for smaller |
| Guarantor personal financial statement | 90 days after fiscal year end | All guarantors |
| Notice of casualty | 5 business days | Damage above $50,000 |
| Notice of litigation | 10 business days | Litigation above $100,000 |

### What Is NOT in the Standard Agency Package (lender additions to flag)

- DSCR covenant testing more frequently than annual
- Additional reserves not in the standard program (lender additions on top of required escrows)
- Restrictions on affiliate management above market rate
- Prohibitions on preferred equity or subordinate financing (supplemental loan provisions are standard; other subordinate debt is not permitted)
- Occupancy covenant below 80% (below 80% is aggressive)

## Covenant Package 2: CMBS

CMBS covenants are somewhat standardized but vary by lender, property type, and securitization structure. The cash management agreement adds another layer of covenants beyond the loan agreement.

### Financial Covenants

| Covenant | Standard Threshold | Testing Frequency | Cure Period | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| DSCR (cash sweep trigger) | 1.15x (trailing 4 quarters) | Quarterly | Cure by depositing additional reserves | Sweep is cash management trigger, not event of default |
| DSCR (event of default) | 1.05x (trailing 4 quarters) | Quarterly | 90 days with lender notice | Below 1.05x for 2+ quarters often triggers special servicing transfer |
| Debt Yield | 7.5-8.5% (property type dependent) | Quarterly | 90 days | DY = annualized NOI / outstanding balance |
| Occupancy (trigger) | 80% physical (varies by property type) | Quarterly | 90 days | Occupancy trigger often accompanies DSCR trigger |
| LTV | 75% (standard) | Annual appraisal (triggered by lender) | Principal paydown | Annual appraisal not always required; lender triggers when concerned |

### Operational Covenants

| Covenant | Standard Threshold | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Lease approval (commercial) | Leases > 5,000 SF or > 5% of GLA require approval | Deemed approval if lender silent for 15 business days |
| Major lease modification | Any modification to approved major lease requires approval | |
| Management company change | Lender approval required | |
| Hotel franchise (if hotel) | Franchise agreement changes require approval | PIP requirements may affect loan structure |
| Ground lease (if applicable) | Ground lessor estoppel required annually | Ground lease amendments require lender consent |
| SPE maintenance | Entity must maintain SPE status throughout loan term | Independent director must remain; separate accounts required |
| Permitted transfers | One-time transfer with approval; assumption fee 0.50-1.00% | |
| Insurance | Replacement value; terrorism insurance if loan > $20M | |
| Environmental | Phase I within 6 months of closing date (reliance period); Phase II if required | |

### Cash Management Covenants (from Cash Management Agreement)

| Provision | Standard | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Lockbox type | Hard lockbox (CMBS standard) | All rents deposited to lockbox account; borrower has no direct access |
| Cash sweep trigger | DSCR < 1.15x for 2 consecutive quarters | Cash swept to lender-controlled excess cash reserve |
| Cure period for sweep | Deposit cash reserve = 6 months debt service | Cash management exits sweep period when DSCR tests above trigger for 2 quarters |
| Distribution conditions | DSCR >= 1.20x + no event of default | Quarterly distribution after cash management waterfall |
| Reserve releases | Lender approval required for reserve draws > $[X] | Tax/insurance releases are automatic at payment dates |

### Reporting Covenants

| Report | Deadline | Format |
|---|---|---|
| Quarterly P&L | 45 days after quarter end | By property; certified by borrower |
| Annual P&L | 90 days after fiscal year end | Audited if loan > $10M |
| Quarterly rent roll | 45 days after quarter end | All commercial tenants; certification of no defaults |
| Tenant estoppels | Within 30 days of lender request | From all tenants above approval threshold |
| Borrower financial statements | 90 days after year end | |
| Guarantor PFS | 90 days after year end | |
| Notice of casualty | 5 business days | |
| Notice of litigation | 5 business days | Above $100,000 threshold |
| Monthly occupancy report (hospitality) | 15 days after month end | RevPAR, ADR, occupancy |

## Covenant Package 3: Bank Balance Sheet

Bank covenants are the most negotiated of any loan type. The ranges below represent typical outcomes; actual terms depend heavily on sponsor relationship, property quality, and negotiating position.

### Financial Covenants

| Covenant | Typical Range | Negotiated Best | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| DSCR | 1.20-1.30x | 1.15x | Semi-annual or annual testing; trailing 12-month |
| LTV | 70-75% | 75% | Annual appraisal required if breach suspected |
| Occupancy | 80-90% | 75-80% | Varies significantly by property type |
| Debt Yield | 8.0-9.0% | 7.5% | Not always present; more common on larger deals |
| Liquid Net Worth (guarantor) | > 1.25x loan amount | 1.0x | Tested annually |
| Liquidity (guarantor) | > 10% of loan amount | 5-10% | Tested annually |

### Operational Covenants

| Covenant | Standard | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Lease approval | > 10% of GLA or $[X] TI | More flexible than CMBS; deemed approval common |
| CapEx approval | > $[X] per item | More flexible than CMBS |
| Management company change | Notice only (no approval) OR approval required | Negotiation point; aim for notice-only |
| Cross-default | Limited to this property and this lender | Red flag if extends to all bank relationships |
| Material adverse change (MAC) | Often included | Flag MAC clause; negotiate narrow definition |
| Permitted debt | No other liens on this property | Trade payables in ordinary course |
| Financial statement delivery | Annual (sometimes quarterly) | Semi-annual is achievable on smaller deals |

### Reporting Covenants

| Report | Standard Deadline | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Annual P&L | 120 days after year end | Negotiated longer than agency/CMBS |
| Semi-annual P&L (large deals) | 60 days after period end | |
| Quarterly rent roll | 45 days after quarter | |
| Annual guarantor PFS | 120 days after year end | |
| Annual borrower financial statements | 120 days after year end | |
| Notice of casualty | 5-10 business days | |
| Notice of material litigation | 10-15 business days | |

## Covenant Package 4: Bridge (Debt Funds / Mortgage REITs)

Bridge loans have the most limited financial covenant package because the borrower is in transition -- financial covenants are not testable during renovation. Bridge covenants are primarily structural (operating the property and funding the renovation correctly) rather than financial (hitting DSCR or occupancy targets during renovation).

### Financial Covenants (Minimal During Renovation Period)

| Covenant | Standard | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| DSCR | Usually no DSCR covenant during renovation | DSCR test activates at stabilization (extension condition) |
| As-stabilized DSCR (extension condition) | 1.10-1.15x | Tested on as-stabilized pro forma, not trailing |
| Minimum occupancy (extension condition) | 80-85% executed leases | Measured on executed leases, not LOIs |
| Minimum liquidity (guarantor) | 5-10% of loan amount | Ongoing liquidity requirement during loan term |

### Operational Covenants

| Covenant | Standard | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Construction/renovation completion | Must complete within [X] months | Tied to loan maturity or extension condition |
| Approved construction budget | Must follow approved budget; overruns > 10% require approval | |
| Draw conditions | Inspection, lien releases, title continuation | Per draw; see loan agreement disbursement section |
| Management company | Approval required for change | |
| Lease approval | Executed leases required for extension test; approval not always required for individual leases | |
| Rate cap maintenance | Cap must be in force for full term including extensions | Replacement cap required if counterparty downgraded |
| Extension conditions | No default + occupancy + DSCR (as-stabilized) + extension fee | Most critical bridge covenants |

## Covenant Package 5: Construction

Construction loan covenants are the most borrower-restrictive because the lender is funding a project not yet in existence. Controls are heavy during construction; they lighten post-completion.

### Construction-Phase Covenants (during construction period)

| Covenant | Standard | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Construction budget compliance | Must complete within approved budget + 10% contingency | Overruns above contingency require additional equity or lender approval |
| Construction schedule compliance | Must complete within approved schedule + 90-180 days | Schedule overruns beyond buffer require extension request |
| Draw conditions | Inspection + lien releases + title continuation + percentage completion | Per draw; non-negotiable |
| Change order approval | Material changes (> $[X] per change or > 5% of total budget) require approval | |
| Contractor approval | Change of GC requires lender consent | |
| Insurance (builder's risk) | Builder's risk insurance required for full replacement value | |
| Completion guaranty compliance | Guarantor must complete regardless of cost | Full completion guaranty |
| No additional encumbrances | No additional liens on property during construction | |
| Lender inspection rights | Lender (or its inspector) may inspect at any time with [X] days notice | |

### Post-Completion (Mini-Perm) Covenants

After certificate of occupancy, covenants transition to standard bridge or permanent loan covenants:

| Covenant | Standard | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| DSCR (if mini-perm) | 1.10-1.15x as-stabilized | Trailing 12-month testing begins 12 months post-CO |
| Occupancy | 80-85% executed leases | Threshold for recourse burn-off and take-out trigger |
| Recourse burn-off conditions | CO + [X]% occupancy + DSCR > [X.XX]x | Full recourse during construction burns off at stabilization |
| Take-out loan funding | Permanent loan must fund within [X] months of CO | Mini-perm is a bridge to permanent, not a permanent loan |
