# Estoppel Certificate Template Pack

---

## TEMPLATE INSTRUCTIONS (strip before use)

This template pack produces estoppel certificates for three transaction contexts: acquisition, refinancing, and subordination. Each variant shares the same core certification fields; the variant-specific addenda address concerns unique to that transaction type.

**Tone**: Formal legal instrument. Every statement is a certification by the tenant. Ambiguity in an estoppel is worse than a gap -- if the tenant cannot certify something, the field must state "Tenant is unable to certify" with an explanation.

**Required before distributing**: Cross-check every field against the underlying lease, rent roll, and deposit records. An estoppel that contradicts the lease creates a title insurance exception or lender holdback.

### Section 13: Lender-Specific Certifications

Tenant acknowledges and certifies:

1. This Certificate is delivered for the benefit of [Lender Name] in connection with a loan secured by the Property.
2. Tenant will not modify, amend, or terminate the Lease without prior written consent of Lender during the term of the Loan, except as expressly permitted under the Lease.
3. If Lender or its successor acquires title through foreclosure or deed in lieu, Tenant agrees to attorn to such successor landlord provided such successor landlord agrees to honor the Lease.
4. Tenant's obligation to pay rent is not subject to any offset, deduction, or counterclaim except as disclosed in Section 9 above.

# VARIANT C: SUBORDINATION, NON-DISTURBANCE, AND ATTORNMENT (SNDA) ADDENDUM

**Purpose**: Typically accompanies an acquisition or refinancing estoppel when the lender requires tenants to subordinate their leasehold interest. This addendum is a negotiated document; the template below represents a balanced starting position.

---

## SUBORDINATION, NON-DISTURBANCE, AND ATTORNMENT AGREEMENT

**Date**: [Date]
**Parties**:
- **Lender**: [Lender Entity Name]
- **Landlord**: [Landlord Entity Name]
- **Tenant**: [Tenant Entity Name]
**Property**: [Property Address]
**Premises**: [Suite/Unit]

### Article 1: Subordination

Tenant agrees that the Lease and Tenant's rights under the Lease are and shall be subject and subordinate to the lien of the Mortgage held by Lender, and to all renewals, modifications, consolidations, replacements, and extensions thereof.

### Article 2: Non-Disturbance

So long as Tenant is not in default beyond applicable cure periods under the Lease, Lender agrees:

1. Tenant's possession of the Premises shall not be disturbed by Lender in the exercise of any rights under the Mortgage, including foreclosure or deed in lieu thereof.
2. The Lease shall continue in full force and effect as a direct lease between Tenant and the successor landlord.
3. Tenant shall not be joined as a party defendant in any foreclosure action.

### Article 3: Attornment

If Lender or any successor acquires title to the Property through foreclosure or deed in lieu:

1. Tenant shall attorn to such successor as landlord under the Lease.
2. The Lease shall continue in full force and effect.
3. Such successor shall be bound by the terms of the Lease, except that such successor shall not be:
   - Liable for any act or omission of any prior landlord;
   - Subject to any offset or defense arising from acts of any prior landlord;
   - Bound by any rent payment made more than one month in advance;
   - Bound by any amendment or modification of the Lease made without Lender's written consent (if such consent was required under the Mortgage).

### Article 4: Notices

Tenant shall deliver to Lender a copy of any default notice sent to Landlord, simultaneously with delivery to Landlord. Lender shall have the right (but not the obligation) to cure any Landlord default within [30] days after receipt of such notice (or such longer period as may be reasonably required).

### Article 5: Insurance and Casualty Proceeds

In the event of casualty, insurance proceeds shall be applied in accordance with the Mortgage. Tenant's right to terminate the Lease following casualty shall be subject to the terms of the Lease and applicable law; Lender shall not unreasonably interfere with Tenant's termination rights expressly stated in the Lease.
