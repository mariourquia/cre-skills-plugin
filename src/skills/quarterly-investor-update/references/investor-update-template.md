# Quarterly Investor Update Template

---

## TEMPLATE INSTRUCTIONS (strip before use)

This template produces a complete quarterly investor update letter for a CRE fund or portfolio. Instructions appear in [BRACKETS]. Remove all bracketed instructions before distributing.

**Audience**: Limited partners. Assume sophisticated investors who read many GP letters. Differentiate with specificity, not length. LPs want: (1) how is my money doing, (2) what changed since last quarter, (3) what are you doing about problems.

**Tone**: Transparent, data-forward. No promotional language. Acknowledge underperformance directly -- LPs spot evasion instantly and it destroys trust.

**Length**: 4-6 pages for a 3-7 asset portfolio. Longer only if there is a material event (acquisition, disposition, refinancing, impairment).

**Required before distributing**: NAV calculations independently verified by fund administrator. All return figures reconciled to audited financials.

## Appendix A: Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| NAV | Net Asset Value: gross asset value less liabilities |
| NOI | Net Operating Income: EGI less operating expenses, before debt service |
| DSCR | Debt Service Coverage Ratio: NOI / annual debt service |
| LTV | Loan-to-Value: outstanding loan balance / property value |
| IRR | Internal Rate of Return: net of all fees and expenses to LPs |
| Equity Multiple | Total distributions / total invested capital |

## 1. Letter from the General Partner

The portfolio generated $1,247,000 in net operating income during Q4, 4.2% above the quarterly plan of $1,197,000. Aggregate occupancy ended the quarter at 95.1%, up 80bps from 94.3% in Q3. The fund distributed $1.85 per unit during Q4, representing an annualized yield of 7.4% on invested capital.

The outperformance was driven primarily by 142 Ferry Street, which completed its 20-unit renovation program 2 months ahead of schedule and achieved average rents of $1,925/month on 14 newly leased renovated units -- $25/month above the $1,900 underwriting assumption. Offsetting this, 88 Pacific Street experienced a 12% property tax reassessment in Q4, adding $18,000 in annual expense not anticipated in the original underwriting. We are filing a formal tax appeal in Q1 2026 with an expected resolution timeline of 6-9 months.

In Q1 2026, we will complete lease-up of the final 6 renovated units at 142 Ferry, execute 8 lease renewals at 315 Market Street (targeting 3.0% increases), and begin pre-marketing 88 Pacific for a potential Q3 2026 disposition.
