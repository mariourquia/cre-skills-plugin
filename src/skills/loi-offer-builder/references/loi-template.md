# Letter of Intent Template: CRE Acquisition

---

## TEMPLATE INSTRUCTIONS (strip before use)

This template produces a non-binding Letter of Intent for a single-asset CRE acquisition. Three variants are provided: Competitive (tight timelines, larger earnest money, fewer contingencies), Balanced (market-standard terms), and Conservative (longer DD, smaller deposit, more contingencies). Select the variant that matches your positioning, then customize.

**Tone**: Professional, direct. An LOI is a negotiation tool, not a legal document. Keep it to 2-3 pages. Over-lawyering an LOI signals inexperience or bad faith.

**Delivery**: LOIs are typically delivered as PDFs on company letterhead. The cover email matters -- keep it to 3 sentences: who you are, what you're offering, and when you need a response.

## 3. Due Diligence Period

### Competitive Variant

**Due Diligence Period**: 21 calendar days from PSA execution.

Buyer shall have the right to inspect and investigate the Property during the DD Period. If day-one hard deposit is provided, DD period is for information confirmation only; Buyer has already committed capital.

### Balanced Variant

**Due Diligence Period**: 30 calendar days from PSA execution, with one (1) optional 15-day extension upon written notice and payment of $[X] additional non-refundable deposit.

Buyer may terminate the PSA for any reason during the DD Period and receive a full refund of the Earnest Money Deposit. Upon expiration of the DD Period without termination, the Deposit becomes non-refundable except as otherwise provided in the PSA.

### Conservative Variant

**Due Diligence Period**: 45 calendar days from PSA execution, with two (2) optional 15-day extensions upon written notice and payment of $[X] additional non-refundable deposit per extension.

Free-look period: First 14 days of DD Period are a free-look period during which Buyer may terminate for any reason with full refund of Deposit and no obligation to provide reasons.

---

## 4. Closing Timeline

### Competitive Variant

**Closing Date**: 30 calendar days from PSA execution (concurrent with DD Period expiration + 9 days).

Buyer represents that financing is pre-approved or that Buyer will close with cash/balance sheet equity. No financing contingency.

### Balanced Variant

**Closing Date**: 60 calendar days from PSA execution, or 30 days after expiration of the DD Period, whichever is later.

Buyer shall have a financing contingency expiring 45 days from PSA execution. If Buyer is unable to secure financing on commercially reasonable terms by such date, Buyer may terminate with full refund of Deposit.

### Conservative Variant

**Closing Date**: 90 calendar days from PSA execution, or 45 days after expiration of the DD Period, whichever is later.

Buyer shall have a financing contingency expiring 75 days from PSA execution. One (1) 30-day extension available upon written notice if financing delay is attributable to lender processing.

## 6. Property Access and Due Diligence Materials

Seller shall provide Buyer and its agents, consultants, and lenders with:

**Physical Access**:
- Reasonable access to the Property during normal business hours upon 24 hours' notice
- Access to all units, common areas, mechanical rooms, and roof
- Permission to conduct environmental assessments (Phase I; Phase II if warranted)
- Permission to conduct property condition assessments and engineering inspections

**Documents** (within 5 business days of PSA execution):
- Rent roll (current, trailing 12 months)
- Operating statements (trailing 3 years, current YTD)
- Tenant leases and amendments
- Service contracts and vendor agreements
- Property tax bills (trailing 3 years)
- Insurance loss run (trailing 5 years)
- Capital expenditure history (trailing 3 years)
- Title commitment and existing survey
- Environmental reports (Phase I/II if available)
- Zoning confirmation / certificate of occupancy
- Building permits and violations history
- Any pending or threatened litigation

## 8. Representations and Warranties

Seller represents and warrants (to survive closing for [12 / 18] months):

- Seller has authority to enter into this transaction
- No pending or threatened condemnation, litigation, or regulatory action affecting the Property
- No environmental contamination known to Seller
- All material contracts disclosed
- Rent roll is accurate and complete as of the date provided
- No tenant concessions, side agreements, or options not disclosed in writing
- Property is in compliance with all applicable laws, codes, and ordinances
- No pending or contemplated tax reassessment

---

## 9. Conditions to Closing

Buyer's obligation to close is subject to:

1. Satisfactory completion of due diligence (Balanced and Conservative variants)
2. Title insurable with no unacceptable exceptions
3. No material adverse change in the condition of the Property between PSA execution and closing
4. Estoppel certificates from tenants representing [75% / 80% / 90%] of occupied square footage or units
5. [If applicable] Receipt of financing commitment on commercially reasonable terms
6. Seller's representations and warranties remaining true and correct as of closing
7. No new leases, lease modifications, or capital commitments by Seller after PSA execution without Buyer's prior written consent

---

## 10. Confidentiality

The terms of this LOI and the existence of this transaction are confidential. Neither party shall disclose any terms to third parties other than their respective advisors, lenders, attorneys, and accountants, each of whom shall be bound by this confidentiality obligation, without the prior written consent of the other party.

## Variant Selection Guide

| Factor | Competitive | Balanced | Conservative |
|---|---|---|---|
| Best for | Marketed deals, multiple bidders, institutional sellers | Most transactions, bilateral negotiations | Off-market deals, complex assets, estate sales |
| Earnest money | 3-5% | 1-2% | 0.5-1% |
| DD period | 21 days, no extension | 30 days + 15-day extension | 45 days + 2x15-day extensions |
| Deposit hard date | Day 1 or end of DD | End of DD | End of DD |
| Financing contingency | None | Yes, 45-day expiry | Yes, 75-day expiry |
| Closing timeline | 30-45 days | 60 days | 90 days |
| When to avoid | Complex environmental, unknown title issues | Institutional sellers with tight timelines | Hot markets with multiple offers |
