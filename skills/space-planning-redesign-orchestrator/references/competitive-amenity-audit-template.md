## Part 4: Rent Premium Correlation Analysis

### Methodology

Compare asking rents across the competitive set, controlling for non-amenity factors (location, age, class, SF), to isolate the rent premium attributable to amenities.

|---|---|---|---|

Calculate: **Implied rent per amenity point** = (Rent delta / Amenity score delta) across the competitive set.

### Step 3: Estimate Achievable Premium

- **Current gap**: Subject amenity score vs. competitive set average.
- **Post-investment score**: Subject amenity score after proposed improvements.
- **Implied premium**: (Score improvement) x (rent per amenity point).
- **Confidence adjustment**: Discount by 25-50% for estimation uncertainty.
- **Market ceiling**: Premium cannot exceed highest-rent comp in the set.

## Part 5: Data Collection Methods

| Method | Reliability | Cost | Best For |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Property tours** | High | Free-Low (time only) | Quality ratings, layout, condition, photos |
| **Broker intelligence** | Medium-High | Free (relationship) | Asking rents, concessions, pipeline, tenant feedback |
| **CoStar / CompStak** | High (quantitative) | Subscription | Rent comps, occupancy, building specs |
| **Mystery shopping** | High | Low (time) | Tenant experience, leasing pitch, tour quality |
| **Tenant interviews** | High (qualitative) | Low (time) | Usage patterns, satisfaction, switching triggers |
| **Online reviews** | Medium | Free | Resident sentiment (multifamily), reputation |
| **Building websites/apps** | Medium | Free | Marketing claims, amenity photos, pricing |
| **Public permit filings** | Medium | Free | Planned renovations at competing properties |

### Best Practices

- Tour competing properties within a 2-week window to ensure contemporaneous comparison.
- Photograph amenity spaces during tours for side-by-side comparison (request permission).
- Cross-reference broker intel with CoStar data to identify discrepancies.
- Check building permit databases for competitor renovation plans that may change the competitive landscape.
- Interview 2-3 tenants who recently moved FROM the subject property to a competitor to understand switching drivers.

### Current Position

- **Rent position**: [above/at/below] market average

### Recommended Strategy

Select one:

1. **Catch-up**: Subject is trailing the set. Invest to close gaps on the 2-3 highest-impact amenities. Goal: reach competitive parity.
2. **Leapfrog**: Subject is near parity. Invest in 1-2 differentiating amenities to create competitive advantage. Goal: justify premium rents.
3. **Maintain**: Subject leads the set. Invest only in refresh and maintenance. Goal: protect position without overcapitalizing.
4. **Reposition**: Subject trails significantly. Major repositioning required across multiple amenities. Goal: change property class perception.
