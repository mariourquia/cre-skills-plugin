# Unit Turnover Budget Template Reference

Make-ready budget templates by unit condition, line-item cost ranges, vendor scheduling framework, and days-vacant tracking. All cost ranges reflect national averages for mid-market multifamily properties (Class B/C) as of mid-2025. Actual costs vary significantly by metro area -- expect 30-60% premiums in NYC, SF, Boston, LA, and DC markets.

---

  If renovation costs $18,675 (mid-range) and enables a $200/month rent increase:

  ROI > cost of capital (mortgage rate or opportunity cost).

## 3. Vendor Scheduling Framework

### Logical Sequencing (critical path)

```
RULE: Work flows top to bottom. Never schedule a downstream trade before
its upstream dependency is complete.

Sequence:
  1. DEMO (if needed) -- must complete before anything else starts
     Remove old flooring, cabinets, fixtures, debris
     Duration: 0.5-1 day for cosmetic, 1-2 days for full reno

  2. ROUGH TRADES -- plumbing, electrical, HVAC (if walls are open)
     Move supply lines, add outlets, reroute ductwork
     Duration: 1-2 days per trade
     Note: only needed if layout changes or upgrades require wall access

  3. DRYWALL -- patch holes, skim coat, repair water damage
     Must complete before painting
     Duration: 1 day (plus dry time for compound -- 24 hours)

  4. PAINT -- always before new flooring (drips on old floor, not new)
     Primer + 2 coats on walls, 1-2 coats on ceiling, trim
     Duration: 2-3 days (including dry time between coats)

  5. CABINETS / COUNTERTOPS -- after painting, before flooring
     Install cabinets first, then template and install countertops
     Note: countertop fabrication (quartz/granite) takes 5-10 business days
     after template -- order early
     Duration: 1-2 days cabinets, 1 day countertops

  6. FLOORING -- after painting and cabinets, before fixtures
     Install LVP, carpet, or tile
     Duration: 1-2 days for 850 SF (LVP or carpet), 2-3 days (tile)

  7. FIXTURES / HARDWARE -- after flooring
     Light fixtures, faucets, toilet, towel bars, door hardware
     Duration: 0.5-1 day

  8. APPLIANCES -- after countertops and final plumbing/electrical connections
     Deliver, install, test all appliances
     Duration: 0.5 day

  9. CLEANING -- always last
     Deep clean all surfaces after all construction dust and debris
     Duration: 0.5-1 day

  10. FINAL INSPECTION + PHOTOS -- after cleaning
      Walk every room, test every fixture, verify punch list complete
      Duration: 1-2 hours

COMMON SCHEDULING MISTAKES:
  - Painting after flooring (drips on new floors = damage or re-clean)
  - Cleaning before all work is done (gets dirty again, wasted money)
  - Scheduling countertop install without accounting for fabrication lead time
  - Not coordinating plumber and electrician for final connections (both needed
    on the same day for appliance hookup)
  - Starting flooring before paint is fully dry (adhesive issues, footprints)
```

## 4. Days-Vacant Tracking and Cost Analysis

### Vacancy Cost Calculator

```
VACANCY COST = (monthly_rent / 30) * days_vacant + make_ready_cost

Example:
  Monthly rent: $1,800
  Daily vacancy cost: $1,800 / 30 = $60/day
  Make-ready cost: $3,765 (Level 2 standard turnover, mid-range)
  Days vacant: 21 days (7 make-ready + 14 marketing/lease-up)
  Total vacancy cost: ($60 * 21) + $3,765 = $1,260 + $3,765 = $5,025

Benchmarks by market type:
  | Market | Average Days Vacant | Target | Concern |
  |---|---|---|---|
  | High-demand urban | 14-21 days | < 14 | > 30 |
  | Suburban | 21-30 days | < 21 | > 45 |
  | Secondary market | 30-45 days | < 30 | > 60 |
  | Rural | 45-60+ days | < 45 | > 90 |

VACANCY REDUCTION STRATEGIES:
  1. Start marketing during notice period (if lease allows showing with notice)
  2. Overlap: accept applications before move-out, screen immediately
  3. Pre-inspect unit during notice period to scope make-ready early
  4. Pre-order materials (paint, flooring) before move-out day
  5. Schedule vendors before move-out (confirm day after move-out)
  6. Offer move-in incentive ($100-200 off first month) for immediate occupancy
  7. Price correctly: overpricing by 5% can add 30+ days of vacancy
     (30 days of vacancy at $1,800/month = $1,800 lost revenue,
      which exceeds the annual value of a 5% rent premium of $1,080)

TURNOVER TRACKING LOG:
  | Unit | Move-Out | Make-Ready Start | Make-Ready End | Listed | Leased | Move-In | Days Vacant | Cost |
  |---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
  | 1A | 01/31 | 02/01 | 02/08 | 02/08 | 02/15 | 03/01 | 29 | $4,465 |
  | 2B | 03/15 | 03/16 | 03/20 | 03/20 | 03/25 | 04/01 | 17 | $1,745 |
```
