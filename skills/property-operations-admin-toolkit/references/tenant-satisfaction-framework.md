# Tenant Satisfaction Survey Framework

## Survey Design

### 15-Question Instrument Across 5 Categories

**Category 1: Maintenance Responsiveness** (3 questions)

| # | Question | Scale | Notes |
|---|----------|-------|-------|
| Q1 | How satisfied are you with the response time when you submit a maintenance request? | 1-5 | Measures speed of acknowledgment |
| Q2 | How satisfied are you with the quality of maintenance work completed in your suite or common areas? | 1-5 | Measures workmanship and first-time fix rate |
| Q3 | How satisfied are you with the communication you receive about the status of your maintenance requests? | 1-5 | Measures proactive updates |

**Category 2: Cleanliness and Appearance** (3 questions)

| # | Question | Scale | Notes |
|---|----------|-------|-------|
| Q4 | How would you rate the overall cleanliness of the building common areas (lobby, restrooms, corridors)? | 1-5 | Core janitorial quality measure |
| Q5 | How would you rate the exterior appearance of the property (landscaping, parking areas, building facade)? | 1-5 | Curb appeal and pride of ownership |
| Q6 | How would you rate the cleanliness and condition of the elevators? | 1-5 | High-visibility, high-frequency touchpoint |

**Category 3: Communication and Management** (3 questions)

| # | Question | Scale | Notes |
|---|----------|-------|-------|
| Q7 | How satisfied are you with the responsiveness and professionalism of the property management team? | 1-5 | Relationship quality measure |
| Q8 | How well does the management team communicate building updates, planned maintenance, and events? | 1-5 | Proactive communication measure |
| Q9 | How satisfied are you with how the management team handles emergencies and after-hours issues? | 1-5 | Crisis response from tenant perspective |

**Category 4: Amenities and Services** (3 questions)

| # | Question | Scale | Notes |
|---|----------|-------|-------|
| Q10 | How satisfied are you with the building's HVAC system (temperature, air quality)? | 1-5 | #1 complaint category in most buildings |
| Q11 | How satisfied are you with the parking facilities (availability, condition, security)? | 1-5 | Critical for suburban and some urban |
| Q12 | How satisfied are you with the building's security and access control systems? | 1-5 | Safety perception |

**Category 5: Value and Overall Satisfaction** (3 questions)

| # | Question | Scale | Notes |
|---|----------|-------|-------|
| Q13 | Considering your total occupancy cost, how would you rate the overall value of your lease? | 1-5 | Price-value alignment (lease renewal predictor) |
| Q14 | How likely are you to renew your lease when it expires? | 1-5 | Direct retention predictor |
| Q15 | How likely are you to recommend this building to a colleague or business associate? | 0-10 (NPS) | Net Promoter Score (industry standard) |

### Scale Definitions

**Satisfaction scale (Q1-Q14)**:
- 5 = Excellent (exceeds expectations)
- 4 = Good (meets expectations)
- 3 = Fair (marginally acceptable)
- 2 = Poor (below expectations)
- 1 = Unacceptable (significantly below expectations)

**NPS scale (Q15)**:
- 9-10 = Promoter
- 7-8 = Passive
- 0-6 = Detractor
- NPS = % Promoters - % Detractors (range: -100 to +100)

### Open-Ended Questions (Append to Survey)

- "What is the one thing we could do to improve your experience in this building?"
- "Is there anything else you would like to share with the management team?"

## Administration Methodology

### Timing

| Property Type | Recommended Timing | Rationale |
|--------------|-------------------|-----------|
| Office | Q1 (January-February) | Covers full prior calendar year, before annual budget discussions |
| Retail | Q1 (February-March) | After holiday season has concluded |
| Industrial | Q1 (January-February) | Same as office |
| Multifamily | Q4 (October-November) | Before renewal season (many leases expire Q1-Q2) |

### Distribution Methods

| Method | Response Rate | Cost | Best For |
|--------|-------------|------|----------|
| Online survey (email link) | 25-40% | Low | Office, tech-savvy tenants |
| Paper survey (delivered to suite) | 30-50% | Moderate | Mixed tenants, older buildings |
| In-person tablet (lobby intercept) | 40-60% | Moderate | High-traffic lobbies, retail |
| Combined (email + paper follow-up) | 40-60% | Moderate | Best overall approach |

### Achieving Target Response Rate (40%+)

1. **Pre-survey notification**: Email from property manager 1 week before launch announcing survey
2. **Launch**: Send survey with personalized introduction
3. **Reminder 1**: 5 business days after launch to non-respondents
4. **Reminder 2**: 10 business days after launch to non-respondents
5. **Final reminder**: 15 business days with "closing tomorrow" urgency
6. **Incentive**: Consider drawing for building amenity credit ($100-250 Starbucks/lunch credit)
7. **Champion approach**: Ask tenant primary contacts to encourage their teams

### Anonymity

- Surveys should be anonymous to encourage honest feedback
- Collect suite/floor number (not individual name) for actionable follow-up
- Clearly state anonymity in survey introduction
- Exception: if tenant requests follow-up, provide optional contact field

| Value | 3.6 | 4.0+ | 3.2 or below |
| NPS (office) | +15 | +30+ | 0 or below |

### Interpretation Guide

| Your Score vs. BOMA | Interpretation | Action |
|---------------------|---------------|--------|
| > Top quartile | Competitive advantage; maintain and promote | Continue investment; use in leasing materials |
| Median to top quartile | Solid performance; targeted improvement | Identify 1-2 areas for advancement |
| Bottom quartile to median | Below market; risk of tenant loss | Immediate action plan; invest in weak areas |
| < Bottom quartile | Critical; active retention risk | Emergency remediation; consider management changes |

## Worked Example: 200-Unit Multifamily Survey with Action Plan

### Property Profile

- **Property**: Maple Grove Apartments
- **Units**: 200 (180 occupied, 90% occupancy)
- **Class**: B+, suburban, built 2008
- **Surveyed**: 180 resident contacts
- **Responses**: 81 (45% response rate)

### Results Summary

| Category | Score | Prior Year | BOMA Median | Status |
|----------|-------|-----------|-------------|--------|
| Maintenance Responsiveness | 3.4 | 3.6 | 3.8 | Below median, declining |
| Cleanliness & Appearance | 4.1 | 4.0 | 3.9 | Above median, improving |
| Communication & Mgmt | 3.9 | 3.8 | 3.7 | Above median, improving |
| Amenities & Services | 3.2 | 3.3 | 3.5 | Below median, declining |
| Value & Satisfaction | 3.6 | 3.7 | 3.6 | At median, declining |
| **Overall** | **3.6** | **3.7** | **3.7** | **Slightly below median** |
| **NPS** | **+8** | **+12** | **+15** | **Below median, declining** |

### Key Findings

1. **Maintenance responsiveness dropped 0.2 points** -- open-ended comments cite "slow response to HVAC requests" and "have to follow up multiple times." Work order data confirms: average completion time increased from 3.2 to 4.8 days.

2. **HVAC satisfaction (Q10) scored 2.8** -- lowest individual question score. 23 of 81 respondents (28%) scored it 1 or 2. Building is 18 years old; original HVAC units reaching end of life.

3. **Cleanliness improved** -- positive response to new landscaping contractor hired Q2 last year and lobby renovation completed Q3.

4. **Value perception declining** -- rent increases of 4.5% vs. perceived improvement gap. Comments: "raising rent but not fixing my AC."

### Action Plan

**Priority 1: HVAC System** (Score: 2.8, Importance: High, Fix: High/Capital)
- Immediate: Hire supplemental HVAC tech for peak season (May-September). Cost: $45,000.
- 30 days: Complete audit of all 200 units -- identify units with end-of-life equipment.
- 90 days: Develop 3-year capital replacement plan with phased unit replacement (est. $4,000/unit x 60 units Year 1 = $240,000).
- Communication: Letter to all residents acknowledging HVAC feedback and outlining improvement plan.

**Priority 2: Maintenance Response Time** (Score: 3.4, Importance: High, Fix: Medium)
- Immediate: Implement automated acknowledgment within 1 hour of work order submission.
- 30 days: Add third maintenance technician (currently 2 for 200 units; industry standard is 1 per 75 units). Cost: $52,000/year.
- 60 days: Implement priority triage system: emergency (<4 hours), urgent (<24 hours), routine (<5 days).
- Metric: Reduce average completion time from 4.8 days to 3.0 days within 90 days.

**Priority 3: Value Perception** (Score: 3.6, Importance: High, Fix: Medium)
- 30 days: Publish "improvements completed" flyer showing all capital investments made in past 12 months.
- 60 days: Add 2 resident amenities at modest cost: package locker system ($15,000) and bike storage ($5,000).
- Ongoing: Ensure Priorities 1 and 2 are visibly progressing before next rent increase cycle.

### Communication Plan

| Date | Action | Audience | Channel |
|------|--------|----------|---------|
| Week 1 | "Thank you for your feedback" summary | All residents | Email + lobby posting |
| Week 2 | "Here's what we heard and what we're doing" | All residents | Email with action plan highlights |
| Month 3 | Progress update on HVAC and maintenance | All residents | Email |
| Month 6 | Mid-year pulse survey (5 questions) | All residents | Email survey |
| Month 9 | Pre-annual survey update on improvements | All residents | Email |

### Financial Summary

| Investment | Annual Cost | Expected Impact |
|------------|------------|-----------------|
| Supplemental HVAC tech | $45,000 | Reduce HVAC complaints 40% during peak |
| HVAC capital replacement (Year 1) | $240,000 | Permanent improvement for 60 units |
| Third maintenance tech | $52,000 | Reduce response time to target |
| Package lockers | $15,000 (one-time) | Amenity improvement |
| Bike storage | $5,000 (one-time) | Amenity improvement |
| **Total Year 1** | **$357,000** | **Target: NPS from +8 to +20** |

**ROI justification**: Current turnover rate is 45% (81 units/year). Average turnover cost: $4,500/unit (lost rent, make-ready, leasing). If improvements reduce turnover by 10 points to 35%: 20 fewer turnovers x $4,500 = $90,000 annual savings + higher rent growth capacity from improved satisfaction.
