# Term Sheet Templates by Loan Type

Annotated term sheet templates for each major CRE loan type. Each section includes the standard market position and the key negotiation lever for that term. Use these as the starting baseline; actual terms depend on lender, property quality, market, and sponsor strength.

---

## Template 1: Agency Multifamily (Freddie Mac / Fannie Mae)

**Use when**: stabilized multifamily, 5+ units, LTV 65-80%, DSCR 1.20-1.35x+.

```
=================================================================
         AGENCY MULTIFAMILY TERM SHEET (INDICATION)
         [Freddie Mac Optigo / Fannie Mae DUS]
=================================================================

BORROWER:           [SPE LLC, state of formation]
GUARANTOR:          [Key principal(s) -- bad-boy only]
PROPERTY:           [Address, unit count, year built, class]
LOAN AMOUNT:        $[X] ([X]% LTV)
LOAN PURPOSE:       [Acquisition / Refinance]
PROGRAM:            [Freddie Optigo / Fannie DUS / Freddie SBL]

-- RATE & STRUCTURE --

Rate Type:          Fixed
Index:              [10-yr / 7-yr / 5-yr Treasury]
Spread:             [X] bps  (see market benchmark by LTV tier below)
All-In Rate:        [X.XX]%
  NOTE: Rate is indicative until rate lock. Locks available at
  application (early lock) or commitment (standard lock).

Rate Lock:          [Standard: 30-90 days at commitment]
                    [Early lock: available at application; cost 10-25 bps]
  Annotation: Early rate locks protect against rising rates during
  a 6-8 week processing period. Worth the cost in rising environments.

Loan Term:          [5 / 7 / 10 / 12 / 15 / 30] years
Amortization:       [30]-year amortization
IO Period:          [0-10] years from closing
  Annotation: Negotiable. Full-term IO available at lower LTV.
  Standard: 1-3 years IO on deals at 65-70% LTV. 5+ years IO
  requires LTV below 65% and DSCR comfort at 1.35x+ amortizing.

Extension Options:  None (fixed-term; prepay to exit)
Origination Fee:    [0.50-1.00]% of loan amount

-- PREPAYMENT --

Structure:          [Yield Maintenance OR Step-Down (1%, declining)]
  Annotation: Freddie step-down is typical on shorter terms (5-7 yr).
  Fannie uses yield maintenance more commonly. Step-down preferred
  for sponsors with uncertain hold periods -- avoids costly YM calc.
Lockout:            [24] months from closing
Step-Down:          Year 3: 5%, Year 4: 4%, Year 5: 3%, Year 6: 2%,
                    Year 7: 1%, thereafter: 0% [adjust per actual term]
  Annotation: Negotiate 1% declining (1% in year after lockout, 0%
  thereafter) vs. standard schedule. Some programs allow open prepayment
  in final 90 days before maturity.

-- RESERVES --

Tax:                2 months upfront + monthly escrow
Insurance:          2 months upfront + monthly escrow
Replacement:        $300-$400/unit/year (multifamily standard)
  Annotation: Negotiate reduction to $250/unit on recently renovated
  properties (< 5 years). Provide capital plan evidence.

-- RECOURSE & GUARANTY --

Recourse:           Non-recourse
Bad-Boy Carve-Outs: Standard (bankruptcy, fraud, waste, misappropriation)
  Annotation: Agency carve-outs are standardized -- Freddie and Fannie
  use prescribed forms. Do not accept expansions to standard carve-out list.
  Environmental indemnity is always unlimited.
Guaranty Burn-Off:  Not available on agency
  Annotation: Unlike bridge lenders, agency lenders do not offer burn-off.
  Guaranty is permanent for loan term.

-- CONDITIONS PRECEDENT --

Lender-Ordered:     Appraisal, Phase I, PCR, flood determination
Borrower-Provided:  Rent roll (all executed leases), financial statements
                    (2 yrs + interim), entity docs, insurance certs,
                    management agreement, SNDA waivers from commercial tenants
                    (if any), certificate of occupancy

-- TIMELINE --

Application:        [date]
Good Faith Deposit: $[X] (applied to costs at closing; forfeited if borrower withdraws)
Processing:         6-8 weeks from complete application
Commitment:         Week [7-9] from application
Rate Lock:          At commitment issuance (standard)
Estimated Closing:  [date]
=================================================================
```

### Agency Spread Benchmarks by LTV Tier

| LTV Tier | Freddie Optigo | Fannie DUS | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| < 55% | T + 140-160 bps | T + 145-165 bps | Lowest-tier pricing |
| 55-65% | T + 155-180 bps | T + 160-185 bps | Standard |
| 65-70% | T + 170-200 bps | T + 175-205 bps | Standard |
| 70-75% | T + 190-225 bps | T + 195-230 bps | Green/Affordable eligible for reduction |
| 75-80% | T + 210-250 bps | T + 215-255 bps | Affordable or Green programs only |

*Benchmarks are mid-2025 approximations. Verify against current program pricing.*

## Template 2: CMBS (Commercial Mortgage-Backed Securities)

**Use when**: office, retail, hotel, mixed-use; non-recourse preferred; loan > $10M; property not eligible for agency.

```
=================================================================
                CMBS TERM SHEET (INDICATION)
=================================================================

BORROWER:           [Bankruptcy-remote SPE -- required]
GUARANTOR:          [Bad-boy only; no completion guaranty]
PROPERTY:           [Address, type, SF, year built, occupancy]
LOAN AMOUNT:        $[X] ([X]% LTV based on appraised value)
LOAN PURPOSE:       [Acquisition / Refinance]
SECURITIZATION:     Will be securitized post-closing. No lender
                    relationship post-securitization; special servicer
                    controls in default.

-- RATE & STRUCTURE --

Rate Type:          Fixed
Index:              [10-yr / 7-yr / 5-yr] Swap or Treasury
Spread:             [X] bps over swap
All-In Rate:        [X.XX]% (fixed for loan term)
Rate Lock:          At application or early processing; 30-90 days
  Annotation: CMBS lenders hedge their rate risk at lock. No float-down
  available. Treasury hedge alternative: partial protection before full lock.

Loan Term:          [5 / 7 / 10] years
Amortization:       [30]-year amortization (or IO-only per IO period)
IO Period:          [12-60] months  [negotiated based on LTV, property quality]
  Annotation: IO period directly improves cash-on-cash return. CMBS IO
  periods have expanded in recent years -- up to 5 years IO on low-LTV deals.
Extension Options:  None (fixed-term CMBS has no extension provisions)
Origination Fee:    [0.50-1.00]%

-- PREPAYMENT --

Structure:          Defeasance (most common on CMBS)
  Annotation: Defeasance replaces the mortgage collateral with government
  securities that replicate the cash flow. Cost depends on Treasury rates
  at exit vs. at origination. In low-rate environment, defeasance is cheap.
  In rising-rate environment, defeasance can be costly (negative arbitrage).
  Alternative: request yield maintenance as an option for loans with uncertain
  exit timing.
Lockout:            First 24 months from closing (open defeasance thereafter)

-- CASH MANAGEMENT --

Lockbox:            [Springing / Hard]
  Annotation: Hard lockbox: all rents flow through lender-controlled account.
  Springing: converts to hard lockbox upon trigger event. Negotiate for
  springing -- hard lockbox requires more operational infrastructure.
Cash Sweep Trigger: DSCR < [1.15x] for [2] consecutive quarters
  Annotation: Negotiate trigger as low as possible (1.10x). At 1.15x, a
  minor NOI fluctuation can trigger sweep and eliminate distributions.
Distribution Test:  DSCR >= [1.20x]; no event of default

-- RESERVES --

Tax:                Monthly escrow required
Insurance:          Monthly escrow required
Replacement:        $[X]/SF/year or $[X]/unit/year
TI/LC Reserve:      $[X] (for office/retail; funded based on near-term rollover)
  Annotation: Negotiate TI/LC reserve based on actual lease expirations,
  not lender formula. Over-reserving ties up capital at closing.
Seasonality:        $[X] (hospitality only)

-- RECOURSE & GUARANTY --

Recourse:           Non-recourse
Bad-Boy Carve-Outs: Standard (see negotiation note below)
  Annotation: CMBS carve-out forms are lender-specific and vary from
  "standard." Review carefully. Common CMBS expansions: transfers without
  consent (strict), modifications without consent, tenant lawsuit cooperation.
Springing Recourse: Full recourse upon: voluntary bankruptcy, unapproved
                    transfers, fraud, misappropriation of rents, waste
  Annotation: Negotiable. Try to limit springing recourse to bad acts,
  not operational performance metrics.

-- SPE REQUIREMENTS (CMBS-SPECIFIC) --

Borrower entity must satisfy CMBS SPE criteria:
  - Single-purpose entity (no other assets or business)
  - Independent director (non-economic) required on board
  - Separate bank accounts, no commingling with affiliates
  - Organizational documents restrict dissolution, merger, transfer
  Annotation: SPE formation requirements add cost. Ensure sponsor's
  operating agreement and entity documents are CMBS-compliant before
  application. Non-compliant entity = deal delay.

-- CONDITIONS PRECEDENT (CMBS-SPECIFIC ADDITIONS) --

All standard items, plus:
  - CMBS-form title insurance endorsements (ALTA 8.1, 9, 15)
  - Zoning report (not just confirmation letter)
  - Operating history (T-12 P&L, T-3 tax returns if entity-level reporting)
  - Franchise agreement (if hotel, requires lender consent to PIP)
  - Ground lease estoppel (if ground lease)
  - SNDAs from all tenants > 5,000 SF (or lender threshold)

-- TIMELINE --

Application:        [date]
Processing:         8-12 weeks (longer than agency due to securitization prep)
Rate Lock:          During processing; 60-90 day window typical
Estimated Closing:  [date]
=================================================================
```

## Template 3: Bridge Loan

**Use when**: value-add, lease-up, transitional properties; 2-3 year hold before permanent financing.

```
=================================================================
               BRIDGE LOAN TERM SHEET (INDICATION)
=================================================================

BORROWER:           [SPE or recourse entity per sponsor credit]
GUARANTOR:          [Bad-boy + completion guaranty if construction]
PROPERTY:           [Address, type, SF, as-is occupancy, stabilized target]
LOAN AMOUNT:        $[X] initial advance + $[X] future funding facility
                    Total commitment: $[X] ([X]% LTC or [X]% of stabilized value)
LOAN PURPOSE:       [Acquisition + Renovation / Refinance + Renovation]

-- RATE & STRUCTURE --

Rate Type:          Floating
Index:              1-month SOFR
Spread:             [SOFR + X bps]  (typical: 300-500 bps depending on LTV/risk)
All-In Rate:        [X.XX]% as of [date] (indicative; adjusts monthly)
Rate Cap:           Required. Strike: SOFR cap at [X]% (all-in capped at [X]%)
                    Term: [loan term + extension period]
                    Minimum counterparty rating: A-/A3
  Annotation: Size rate cap for full extended term. Buying a cap extension
  after the fact costs significantly more than sizing correctly upfront.

Loan Term:          [2-3] years from closing
Extension Options:  [1-2] x [6-12] months
  Extension Conditions (negotiate each):
    1. No monetary event of default
    2. Minimum occupancy: [X]% executed leases (not LOIs)
    3. DSCR test: as-stabilized DSCR > [1.10x] (not trailing 12-month)
    4. Rate cap in place covering extension period
    5. Extension fee: [0.25-0.50]% of outstanding balance
  Annotation: Extension conditions are the most important bridge term.
  Negotiate condition 3 to pro forma/stabilized, not trailing. A value-add
  property under renovation cannot meet trailing DSCR tests.

Origination Fee:    [1.00-1.50]%
Exit Fee:           [0.50-1.00]% (due at payoff or maturity)

-- FUTURE FUNDING FACILITY --

Holdback Amount:    $[X] (renovation / TI / LC budget)
Draw Conditions:    Lender inspection and approval of completed work
                    Lien releases for work completed to date
                    Title continuation (bringdown) each draw
                    Contractor invoices and AIA G702/G703 forms
Draw Schedule:      [Monthly / Bi-monthly] per construction schedule
Interest on Draws:  Charged only on amounts drawn (not on committed holdback)
  Annotation: Confirm that interest is charged on drawn amounts only.
  Some lenders charge commitment fee on undrawn holdback -- negotiate out.

-- PREPAYMENT --

Structure:          Open after [6-12] months; exit fee applies
  Annotation: Open prepayment is critical for bridge. You cannot predict
  exactly when the permanent loan will close. Avoid step-downs or yield
  maintenance on bridge -- they add uncertainty to the refinance timeline.
Exit Fee:           [0.50-1.00]% on full payoff at any time

-- RECOURSE & GUARANTY --

Recourse:           [Non-recourse with carve-outs / Partial recourse]
  Annotation: Non-recourse is achievable for institutional sponsors.
  Smaller sponsors or complex value-add deals may see partial recourse (25-50%).
  Full recourse during construction; burns off at occupancy and DSCR tests.
Completion Guaranty: Required if renovation materially affects occupancy
  (full completion, not just budget guaranty, for gut renovations)
Guaranty Burn-Off:  Upon: [90% occupancy for 90 days AND DSCR > 1.15x]
  Annotation: Burn-off conditions should be achievable on the business plan.
  Negotiate occupancy threshold to 85% and DSCR test to as-stabilized NOI.

-- CASH MANAGEMENT --

Lockbox:            [None during stabilized operation / Springing on default]
Cash Sweep:         Typically not applicable during value-add period
  Annotation: Many bridge lenders do not impose cash management during the
  value-add period -- that's the whole point. Push back on any springing
  lockbox triggered by operating metrics rather than default events.

-- RESERVES --

Interest Reserve:   [X months] funded at closing from loan proceeds
  Annotation: Fund 6-12 months of interest reserve to cover negative cash
  flow during renovation. Lender will insist; size correctly.
Renovation Holdback: See Future Funding Facility above
Operating Reserve:  $[X] for unexpected CapEx or leasing costs

-- CONDITIONS PRECEDENT --

Standard items, plus:
  Construction budget (if renovation): approved by lender's engineer
  Contractor agreement: GC identity, AIA contract form, performance bond
  Rate cap commitment letter at application
=================================================================
```

## Template 4: Construction Loan

**Use when**: ground-up development or major gut renovation requiring building permits.

Key differences from bridge: completion guaranty is full (not budget-only), draws are milestone-based, mini-perm provision, and take-out commitment is lender condition.

```
=================================================================
           CONSTRUCTION LOAN TERM SHEET (INDICATION)
=================================================================

BORROWER:           [Development entity -- often recourse during construction]
GUARANTOR:          [Completion guaranty + carve-out guaranty]
PROJECT:            [Address, product type, unit count / SF, total cost]
LOAN AMOUNT:        $[X] ([X]% LTC -- loan-to-cost, not loan-to-value)
  Annotation: Construction loans are sized to LTC, not LTV. Typical max
  is 65-70% LTC including land (50-60% of land value in some markets).
  Land equity requirement: often 100% of land must be owned free and clear.

-- RATE & STRUCTURE --

Rate Type:          Floating (SOFR + spread)
Spread:             SOFR + [X] bps  (typical: 250-400 bps)
Interest Reserve:   Funded into loan; covers [X months] of construction
  Annotation: Size interest reserve conservatively -- construction delays
  are common. Fund 110-120% of projected interest during construction.
Loan Term:          [18-36] months construction period
Mini-Perm Option:   [X] months following certificate of occupancy
  Annotation: Mini-perm converts construction loan to stabilization loan
  automatically. Allows lease-up without refinancing during construction.
  Critical on speculative (non-pre-leased) projects.
Extension Options:  [1] x [6] months for construction completion delays
  Extension Conditions: force majeure; no material default; lender engineer
  confirms project is on track for completion within extended period.

-- DRAW MECHANICS --

Draw Schedule:      Monthly draws tied to construction milestones
  Milestone examples:
    Foundation complete: 15% of commitment
    Structure complete: 30% of commitment
    Roofing complete: 50% of commitment
    Mechanical / electrical / plumbing rough: 65% of commitment
    Dry wall: 75% of commitment
    Substantial completion: 90% of commitment
    Certificate of occupancy: 100% minus retainage
  Annotation: Negotiate milestone definitions clearly. "Substantial completion"
  should reference a specific punch-list, not lender's discretion.

Draw Conditions (each draw):
  [ ] Lender's inspector approval (usually 3rd-party construction manager)
  [ ] Title continuation (bringdown; no new liens or exceptions)
  [ ] Lien releases from contractor and all sub-contractors
  [ ] AIA G702/G703 application for payment
  [ ] Percentage completion certified by architect
  [ ] Funds control / disbursement agent approval (for large projects)

Retainage:          [5-10]% withheld until final completion
  Released on:      Certificate of occupancy + 30-day lien period clear

-- COMPLETION GUARANTY --

Type:               Full completion guaranty (not budget-only)
  Annotation: Budget guaranty caps guarantor exposure at budget overrun.
  Full completion guaranty requires guarantor to complete the project
  regardless of cost. Know the difference before signing.
Guarantor:          Individuals with net worth > [1.5x] loan amount
Burn-Off:           Upon certificate of occupancy and [X]% pre-leasing

-- TAKE-OUT COMMITMENT --

Lender Requirement: Permanent lender commitment required before or within
                    [60] days of construction loan closing
  Annotation: "Forward commitment" from agency or CMBS lender locks the
  permanent rate and eliminates take-out risk. Adds cost (commitment fee)
  but eliminates worst-case scenario of no permanent financing at completion.
  On pre-leased projects, agency take-out commitment is standard.
  On speculative projects, take-out commitment is difficult to obtain --
  negotiate mini-perm provision to buy time.

-- RECOURSE --

During Construction: Full recourse until certificate of occupancy
  Annotation: Construction lenders universally require full recourse
  during construction. Non-negotiable. Recourse burns off at CO + stabilization.
Post-CO:            Non-recourse with standard carve-outs (if mini-perm)
=================================================================
```

## Template 5: Mezzanine Loan

**Use when**: filling gap between senior loan and required equity; rates 10-15%; term matches or is shorter than senior.

```
=================================================================
              MEZZANINE LOAN TERM SHEET (INDICATION)
=================================================================

BORROWER:           [Mezzanine borrower entity -- pledges 100% of equity
                     interest in senior loan borrower entity as collateral]
SENIOR LOAN:        $[X] (lender: [name]; maturity: [date])
MEZZANINE AMOUNT:   $[X] ([X]% of total capital stack)
TOTAL DEBT:         $[X] ([X]% combined LTV)

-- RATE & STRUCTURE --

Rate Type:          [Fixed / Floating]
Rate:               [X.XX]% (cash pay) or [X.XX]% (PIK + cash pay split)
  PIK (Payment-in-Kind): portion of interest accrues to principal
  Annotation: Sponsors prefer more cash pay / less PIK to control accrual.
  Mezz lenders accept more PIK in return for higher total return.
Loan Term:          [matches or shorter than senior loan term]

-- COLLATERAL --

Pledge:             100% equity interest in senior borrowing entity (UCC lien)
  Annotation: Mezz lender does NOT have mortgage on property. Security is
  equity pledge. In default, mezz lender exercises UCC foreclosure (faster
  than mortgage foreclosure -- 30-60 days vs. 6-18 months). This speed is
  why intercreditor agreement is critical -- senior lender must agree to
  standstill and cure periods.

-- INTERCREDITOR AGREEMENT --

Must be negotiated and agreed by senior lender before mezz closes.
Key provisions:
  1. Notice of senior loan default: mezz lender receives simultaneous notice
  2. Cure rights: mezz lender can cure senior loan default within [30-60 days]
  3. Purchase option: mezz lender can purchase senior loan at par + accrued
     interest if senior loan is in uncured default
  4. Transfer restrictions: mezz lender must obtain senior lender consent
     before transferring mezzanine loan (limits mezz lender flexibility)
  5. Standstill: senior lender standstill of [60-120 days] before foreclosing
     to allow mezz lender to exercise purchase option or cure
  Annotation: Review intercreditor agreement as carefully as loan docs.
  Restrictive intercreditor terms can eliminate mezz lender's enforcement
  rights -- making the mezz loan effectively unsecured.
=================================================================
```
