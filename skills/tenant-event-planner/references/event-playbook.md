# Tenant Event Playbook

Reference catalog of event types, budget templates, vendor categories, and measurement frameworks for CRE tenant events.

## Event Type Catalog

### 1. Tenant Appreciation BBQ / Cookout

- **Best for**: Multifamily, mixed-use
- **Season**: Late spring through early fall (May-September)
- **Budget**: $8-15/unit (MF), $12-18/tenant (mixed-use)
- **Typical attendance**: 40-60% of units
- **Duration**: 3-4 hours
- **Setup**: Outdoor courtyard or pool deck; requires grills (rented or on-site), tables, shade structures
- **Staffing**: 1 coordinator + 1 grill operator per 50 attendees + 1-2 setup/teardown
- **Key items**: Burgers, hot dogs, sides, beverages (non-alcoholic standard; beer/wine with permit and insurance), lawn games, music
- **Dietary note**: Always provide vegetarian and gluten-free options; label all allergens
- **Weather contingency**: Tent rental adds $500-1,500; indoor fallback if available

### 2. Holiday Party

- **Best for**: Office, mixed-use
- **Season**: December (schedule early to mid-December to avoid travel season)
- **Budget**: $15-25/tenant (office), $12-20/unit (MF)
- **Typical attendance**: 60-80% (office), 35-50% (MF)
- **Duration**: 2-3 hours (office evening), 4-6 hours open-house style (MF)
- **Setup**: Lobby, conference center, or rooftop; requires decor transformation, sound system, catering setup
- **Staffing**: 1 coordinator + catering staff + 1 bartender per 75 guests (if alcohol served)
- **Key items**: Catered hors d'oeuvres or buffet, themed decor, photo booth, live music or DJ, gift raffle
- **Dietary note**: Full dietary accommodation required; halal/kosher options in diverse markets
- **Insurance**: Liquor liability rider required if alcohol served ($200-500 for single-event policy)

### 3. Wellness Fair

- **Best for**: Office, Class A multifamily
- **Season**: January (New Year's resolutions), September (back-to-routine)
- **Budget**: $5-10/tenant (office), $8-12/unit (MF)
- **Typical attendance**: 30-50%
- **Duration**: 3-5 hours (drop-in format)
- **Setup**: Lobby or common area with station layout; requires tables, signage, power for vendor equipment
- **Staffing**: 1 coordinator; vendors self-staff their stations
- **Key items**: Health screenings (blood pressure, BMI), chair massage, yoga demo, smoothie bar, fitness class signup, mental health resource table, health insurance info (open enrollment tie-in)
- **Vendor approach**: Many wellness vendors participate free as lead generation; budget covers smoothies, swag, coordination
- **ROI bonus**: Demonstrates property management investment in tenant wellbeing; strong social media content

### 4. Food Truck Friday

- **Best for**: Any property type with accessible parking or courtyard
- **Season**: Spring through fall (weekly or biweekly series)
- **Budget**: $500-1,500/event (property subsidy; tenants may pay for food or property covers a per-person credit)
- **Typical attendance**: 20-35% per event; cumulative reach 60-80% over a series
- **Duration**: 2-3 hours (lunch or early evening)
- **Setup**: Designated parking area or loading zone for truck; requires power access, waste bins, seating
- **Staffing**: Minimal; 1 coordinator for vendor liaison and setup
- **Key items**: Rotating truck lineup (taco, BBQ, Asian fusion, dessert); tenant voting on next truck adds engagement
- **Logistics**: Confirm truck dimensions vs. access points; fire lane compliance; health permit verification
- **Series strategy**: Commit to 8-12 week run for best pricing and tenant habit formation

### 5. Networking Mixer

- **Best for**: Office, co-working, mixed-use with commercial tenants
- **Season**: Quarterly (January kickoff, April spring, September fall, November pre-holiday)
- **Budget**: $10-20/tenant
- **Typical attendance**: 25-40%
- **Duration**: 2 hours (typically 5-7 PM)
- **Setup**: Lobby, rooftop, or tenant lounge; cocktail-height tables, bar setup, ambient lighting
- **Staffing**: 1 coordinator + bartender + 1 server per 50 guests
- **Key items**: Wine/beer/cocktails, passed appetizers, nametags with company/suite, conversation starter cards, optional speaker or panel (tenant spotlight)
- **Value-add**: Facilitates inter-tenant business relationships; document connections made for retention reporting
- **Format variations**: Speed networking, industry-specific, women-in-business, startup showcase

### 6. Move-In Welcome Package

- **Best for**: Multifamily, office
- **Season**: Year-round (triggered by lease commencement)
- **Budget**: $50-100/unit (MF), $100-250/tenant company (office)
- **Typical reach**: 100% of new tenants
- **Duration**: N/A (delivered item)
- **Setup**: Pre-assembled packages stored on-site
- **Key items (MF)**: Welcome letter, local restaurant guide, building amenity overview, small gift (branded tote, candle, snack box), move-in discount coupons from local businesses
- **Key items (office)**: Welcome packet, building directory, conference room booking guide, branded items, coffee/snack basket for the suite, intro to property management team
- **Partnerships**: Local businesses often provide free coupons/samples for inclusion (cross-promotion)
- **Timing**: Deliver within 24 hours of move-in; follow up with personal intro from property manager within 1 week

### 7. Pet-Friendly Event (Yappy Hour)

- **Best for**: Multifamily with pet-friendly policies
- **Season**: Spring and fall (comfortable outdoor temps)
- **Budget**: $5-10/unit
- **Typical attendance**: 25-40% (pet owners overindex on attendance)
- **Duration**: 2 hours
- **Setup**: Courtyard, dog park, or fenced outdoor area; requires waste stations, water bowls, shade
- **Staffing**: 1 coordinator + optional pet photographer
- **Key items**: Dog treats (branded), pet bandanas, local vet or groomer on-site, pet costume contest, adoption partner table, "yappy hour" beverages for humans
- **Insurance**: Confirm general liability covers pet events; consider additional rider
- **Rules**: Vaccination verification, leash requirements, aggressive behavior policy posted

### 8. Kids Back-to-School Event

- **Best for**: Multifamily (family-heavy communities)
- **Season**: Late August / early September
- **Budget**: $8-12/unit
- **Typical attendance**: 50-70% of families with children
- **Duration**: 2-3 hours
- **Setup**: Clubhouse, pool deck, or community room; requires tables for activities and supply distribution
- **Key items**: Backpack and school supply drive (partner with local businesses for donations), face painting, balloon artist, ice cream truck, school bus photo op, resource table for local tutoring/afterschool programs
- **Community impact**: Strong goodwill; local media coverage potential
- **Sponsorship opportunity**: Office supply stores, tutoring companies, children's dentists often sponsor in exchange for visibility

### 9. Charity / Community Service Event

- **Best for**: Any property type
- **Season**: November-December (giving season) or tied to specific causes year-round
- **Budget**: $2-5/tenant (low direct cost; high engagement value)
- **Typical attendance**: 15-30% active participation; 40-60% donation/awareness
- **Duration**: 2-4 hours
- **Setup**: Varies by activity; lobby collection points for drives, outdoor setup for service projects
- **Key items**: Food drive, toy drive, coat drive, blood drive (partner with Red Cross), park cleanup, Habitat for Humanity build day, care package assembly for deployed military or shelter residents
- **Marketing**: Strongest social media and PR content of any event type
- **Tenant engagement**: Invite tenant companies to match donations or volunteer as teams

### 10. Milestone Celebration

- **Best for**: Any property type
- **Season**: Tied to specific milestone date
- **Budget**: $5-15/unit or tenant, depending on milestone significance
- **Typical attendance**: 30-50%
- **Duration**: 1-3 hours
- **Setup**: Lobby or primary common area
- **Milestones**: Building anniversary (1yr, 5yr, 10yr), lease anniversary recognition, occupancy milestone (fully leased), renovation completion, award/certification (LEED, WELL, ENERGY STAR)
- **Key items**: Commemorative gift (branded item, framed photo), cake/champagne toast, photo display of building history, tenant testimonial wall, time capsule (for major milestones)
- **Retention play**: Lease anniversary recognition with small gift ($25-50) drives renewal conversation

### 11. Seasonal Decoration Reveal

- **Best for**: Any property type with significant lobby or common area
- **Season**: November-December (holiday), October (fall), March-April (spring)
- **Budget**: $3-8/unit for the reveal event; decor budget separate (typically $2,000-15,000 depending on property size and class)
- **Typical attendance**: 40-60% (if paired with refreshments and music)
- **Duration**: 1-2 hours (reveal moment + mingling)
- **Setup**: Lobby transformation; requires advance decor installation with reveal covering
- **Key items**: Hot cocoa/cider bar, live carolers or string quartet (holiday), photo opportunity with display, voting on favorite decoration (engagement driver), countdown to reveal moment
- **Social media**: Encourage tenant posting with property hashtag; offer small prize for best photo
- **Series potential**: Monthly lobby art installation rotation with artist receptions

### 12. Outdoor Movie Night

- **Best for**: Multifamily with courtyard, lawn, or rooftop; mixed-use with plaza
- **Season**: Summer (June-August, start time after sunset)
- **Budget**: $1,000-2,500 all-in
- **Typical attendance**: 30-50%
- **Duration**: 3-4 hours (setup + pre-show + movie)
- **Setup**: Inflatable screen rental ($300-600), projector and sound ($200-400), seating (blankets/lawn chairs provided or BYOB), power supply
- **Staffing**: 1 coordinator + 1 A/V tech
- **Key items**: Popcorn machine ($75-150 rental + supplies), candy bar, beverages, blankets for loan, pre-movie trivia or short film
- **Licensing**: Public performance license required (Swank or Criterion; $300-500 per screening); do NOT screen without license
- **Series strategy**: Monthly summer series builds anticipation; tenant voting on film selection drives engagement

## Measurement Framework

### Primary Metrics

1. **Attendance Rate**: Actual attendees / units or tenants invited
   - Target: Varies by event type (see catalog above)
   - Track trend over time; declining attendance signals event fatigue or format staleness

2. **Tenant Satisfaction Lift**: Post-event survey score vs. baseline
   - Measure via 3-question post-event survey (enjoyment, likelihood to attend future, overall satisfaction with property management)
   - Target: 4.0+ on 5-point scale

3. **Retention Correlation**: Lease renewal rate among event attendees vs. non-attendees
   - Requires 12-month trailing data
   - Industry benchmark: 5-10 percentage point higher renewal rate among regular event attendees

4. **Cost Per Attendee**: Total event cost / actual attendees
   - Benchmark: $12-25 for standard events; $25-50 for premium events
   - Track to ensure value delivery

### Secondary Metrics

5. **Social Media Engagement**: Posts, shares, hashtag usage from event
   - Particularly relevant for younger demographics and Class A properties

6. **RSVP Accuracy**: RSVPs / actual attendance
   - Helps calibrate future planning; typical ratio is 0.7-0.85 (overpromise, under-attend)

7. **Net Promoter Score Impact**: Property NPS before/after active event programming
   - Expect 3-8 point NPS lift from consistent quarterly events

8. **Referral Attribution**: New leases citing community/events as a factor
   - Track via leasing survey question: "What attracted you to this property?"
