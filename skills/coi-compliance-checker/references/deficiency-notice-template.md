# COI Deficiency Notice Templates

Reference templates for insurance certificate deficiency notices. Three escalation tiers: initial notice, second notice with cure period, and default notice.

---

### Outstanding Deficiencies

| # | Deficiency | Originally Identified | Days Outstanding |
|---|------------|-----------------------|------------------|
| 1 | [e.g., GL limit below $2M per occurrence] | [FIRST NOTICE DATE] | [N] days |
| 2 | [e.g., Missing additional insured endorsement] | [FIRST NOTICE DATE] | [N] days |
| 3 | [e.g., Missing waiver of subrogation -- WC] | [FIRST NOTICE DATE] | [N] days |

### Cure Period

Pursuant to Section [DEFAULT/CURE SECTION] of the Lease, you are hereby provided a **[CURE PERIOD -- e.g., ten (10)] day cure period** to remedy the above deficiencies. The cure period expires on **[FINAL CURE DEADLINE DATE]**.

### Consequences of Non-Compliance

Failure to cure the above deficiencies by the expiration of the cure period may result in one or more of the following remedies available to Landlord under the Lease:

1. **Landlord Procurement**: Landlord may procure the required insurance coverage on your behalf and charge all premiums, costs, and administrative fees to Tenant as Additional Rent pursuant to Section [SECTION]. Landlord-procured coverage is typically obtained at significantly higher premiums than tenant-procured coverage.

2. **Event of Default**: Continued non-compliance beyond the cure period constitutes an Event of Default under Section [DEFAULT SECTION] of the Lease, entitling Landlord to pursue all remedies available under the Lease, including but not limited to monetary damages and lease termination.

3. **Indemnification Exposure**: Absent compliant insurance, Tenant bears unlimited personal exposure for all claims, damages, and losses arising from or related to the Premises, including defense costs that would otherwise be covered by the required policies.

## Usage Notes

1. **Timing**: Initial notice at first identification. Second notice 15-20 days after initial. Default notice after cure period expiration.
2. **Delivery**: Always use certified mail for legal enforceability. Email is supplemental, not a substitute for formal delivery unless the lease explicitly permits it.
3. **Lease review**: Confirm the lease's specific cure periods, notice addresses, and default provisions before sending. Cure periods vary (commonly 10-30 days for non-monetary defaults).
4. **Lender notice**: Some loan documents require landlord to notify lender of material tenant defaults. Check the loan agreement.
5. **State law**: Some jurisdictions impose statutory notice requirements that override lease provisions. Confirm local requirements.
6. **Record keeping**: Log all notices in the tenant insurance tracking system with dates sent, delivery confirmation, and response dates.
