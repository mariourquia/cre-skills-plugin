# Fund Terms Guide: GP Commitment, Key Person, Fee Structures, Reg D

---

## Purpose

This reference covers the economic and governance terms that define a CRE fund's relationship between the GP and LPs. Each section includes market norms, negotiation dynamics, and specific guidance for emerging managers ($25-150M fund size).

---

## Part 1: GP Commitment

### 1.1 Market Norms

| Fund Size | Typical GP Commitment | Range | Notes |
|-----------|----------------------|-------|-------|
| <$50M | 1-3% | $250K-$1.5M | Often represents majority of GP's liquid net worth |
| $50-150M | 2-5% | $1-7.5M | Institutional LPs push for 3%+ |
| $150-500M | 3-5% | $4.5-25M | Standard institutional range |
| $500M+ | 1-3% | $5-15M | Dollar amount matters more than percentage |

### 1.2 What Counts as GP Commitment

| Source | Counts? | LP Perception | Notes |
|--------|---------|---------------|-------|
| Cash from GP personal funds | Yes -- strongest signal | Excellent | Demonstrates skin in the game |
| GP management fee waiver | Technically yes | Moderate | LPs view this as circular (paying yourself less) |
| GP co-invest alongside fund (same deal terms) | Yes | Good | Must be same economics as LP |
| Carried interest reinvestment | No | Poor | GP is investing profits, not capital at risk |
| Third-party loan to GP for commitment | Generally no | Poor | Leverage on GP commitment defeats the purpose |
| GP entity retained earnings | Yes | Good | If the entity has meaningful accumulated capital |

### 1.3 Negotiation Dynamics

**LP position**: "We want at least 5% GP commitment to ensure alignment."

**GP response framework**:
1. State the dollar amount and its personal significance
2. Show other alignment features: clawback, key person, no-fault removal
3. Offer to increase GP commitment if fund exceeds target size
4. If pressed, offer to fund from management fee reduction (fee offset)

**Emerging manager reality**: A first-time fund manager raising $50M may have $500K-1.5M of personal liquid capital. Committing $2.5M (5%) may require pledging illiquid assets. LPs generally understand this, but the GP should explain it transparently rather than hoping the LP does not ask.

### 1.4 GP Commitment Documentation

The LPA should specify:
- Dollar amount of GP commitment (or percentage of total commitments)
- Timing of GP funding (pro-rata with LPs, or front-loaded)
- Whether GP commitment is subject to capital calls or funded upfront
- Source of GP commitment (personal funds, entity capital, management fee waiver)
- Consequences if GP fails to fund its commitment

## Part 2: Key Person Provisions

### 2.1 Standard Structure

| Element | Market Standard | Emerging Manager Consideration |
|---------|----------------|-------------------------------|
| Named key persons | 1-3 individuals | Usually the founding partners |
| Time commitment | "Substantially all business time" | Define: typically 75%+ |
| Trigger event | Death, disability, departure, or failure to devote substantially all time | Include incapacitation |
| Consequence | Investment period automatically suspended | Not terminated -- suspended |
| LP remedy | LP vote to resume or wind down (typically 2/3 or 75% vote) | 90-day decision period is standard |
| Cure period | 60-180 days to find replacement | GP needs time to recruit |

### 2.2 Key Person Event Workflow

```
Day 0:   Key Person event occurs
Day 1-5: GP notifies LPs in writing
Day 5:   Investment period automatically suspended
         (No new investments; existing investments continue to be managed)
Day 5-30: GP proposes replacement or remediation plan
Day 30:  GP formally presents plan to LPAC and all LPs
Day 30-120: LP vote to resume or wind down
           - Resume: investment period restarts with new key person
           - Wind down: fund enters harvesting mode (manage and dispose existing assets)
```

### 2.3 Negotiation Points

**LP concern**: "What if both key persons leave?"

**GP considerations**:
- Having 2 key persons (not 1) reduces concentration risk
- Consider a "key person lite" provision: loss of 1 of 2 triggers a consultation period, not automatic suspension
- Advisory committee should have input on replacement approval
- GP should maintain relationships with potential operating partners who could step in

**Emerging manager trap**: Do not agree to a key person provision that names only one person and provides no cure period. This makes the fund uninvestable if that person has a temporary health issue. Insist on a cure period and a reasonable definition of "substantially all."

## Part 3: Fee Structures

### 3.1 Management Fee

| Parameter | Market Range | Notes |
|-----------|-------------|-------|
| Rate during investment period | 1.0-2.0% | 1.5% most common for emerging value-add |
| Basis during investment period | Committed capital | LPs prefer "invested" -- GP prefers "committed" |
| Rate post-investment period | 0.75-1.5% | Typically step-down of 25-50bps |
| Basis post-investment period | Invested capital (cost or lesser of cost/NAV) | Always switches to invested |
| Calculation frequency | Quarterly in advance | Some LPAs use semi-annual |
| Fee offsets | 0-100% of deal-level fees offset management fee | 100% offset is LP-friendly standard |

**Committed vs. Invested**: On a $50M fund with a 3-year investment period, committed-based fees generate $2.25M in year 1 (1.5% x $50M) regardless of deployment pace. Invested-based fees depend on capital deployed, which may be $10-15M in year 1, generating $150-225K. This is a significant difference for the GP's operating budget.

**Emerging manager economics**: At 1.5% on $50M committed, the GP generates $750K/year in management fees. After fund expenses (legal, accounting, office, travel), the GP may net $300-400K before team compensation. This is why GPs resist fee reductions below 1.25% on small funds.

### 3.2 Acquisition Fee

| Parameter | Market Range | Notes |
|-----------|-------------|-------|
| Rate | 0.5-1.5% | Of purchase price or total capitalization |
| Basis | Purchase price (most common) | Some LPAs use total project cost including capex |
| Offset | 50-100% against management fee | 100% offset is the institutional standard |
| Paid by | Fund or deal-level SPE | Varies |

### 3.3 Disposition Fee

| Parameter | Market Range | Notes |
|-----------|-------------|-------|
| Rate | 0.5-1.0% | Of gross sale price |
| Standard | Many institutional LPs push to eliminate | Common for emerging managers to retain |
| Offset | 50-100% | If not eliminated |

### 3.4 Carried Interest

| Parameter | Market Range | Notes |
|-----------|-------------|-------|
| Rate | 20% | Overwhelmingly standard |
| Preferred return (hurdle) | 7-9% | 8% is most common for value-add CRE |
| Compounding | Annual or quarterly | Quarterly compounding is more LP-friendly |
| Catch-up | 50-100% to GP after pref is met | 100% = GP receives all distributions until caught up to 20% of total |
| Basis | Deal-by-deal or whole-fund | Whole-fund is more LP-friendly (no cherry-picking) |
| Clawback | Yes (standard) | GP returns excess carry at fund wind-down |
| Clawback security | Escrow (25-50% of carry), personal guarantee, or unsecured | Escrow is strongest |

### 3.5 Waterfall Mechanics

**American Waterfall (Deal-by-Deal)**:
```
For each realized investment:
1. Return LP capital invested in that deal
2. Pay LP preferred return (8% compounded) on that deal
3. GP catch-up to 20% of cumulative distributions on that deal
4. 80/20 LP/GP split
```
Pros for GP: Carry paid earlier. Cons for LP: GP may receive carry on winning deals before losing deals are realized.

**European Waterfall (Whole-Fund)**:
```
Across all investments:
1. Return ALL LP capital (all deals, fees, expenses)
2. Pay LP preferred return (8% compounded) on ALL capital
3. GP catch-up to 20% of cumulative distributions
4. 80/20 LP/GP split
```
Pros for LP: GP receives no carry until ALL capital is returned with preferred return. LP is better protected against aggregate losses.

### 3.6 Fee Table Summary (Emerging Manager, $50M Value-Add)

| Fee | Rate | Basis | Offset | Annual Estimate |
|-----|------|-------|--------|----------------|
| Management fee | 1.50% | Committed (IP) / Invested (post-IP) | N/A | $750K (IP) |
| Acquisition fee | 1.00% | Purchase price | 100% offset vs. mgmt fee | $120K per deal |
| Disposition fee | 0.50% | Sale price | None | $65K per deal |
| Construction mgmt | 5.00% | Hard cost budget | None | $18K per deal |
| Carried interest | 20.00% | Above 8% pref, whole-fund | N/A | TBD at realization |

## Part 4: Reg D Compliance Checklist

### 4.1 Overview

Most CRE funds rely on Regulation D exemptions from SEC registration. The two most common exemptions:

| Exemption | Rule 506(b) | Rule 506(c) |
|-----------|------------|------------|
| General solicitation allowed? | No | Yes |
| Investor types | Accredited investors + up to 35 non-accredited | Accredited investors ONLY |
| Verification of accreditation | Self-certification (checkbox) | Must take "reasonable steps" to verify |
| Offering method | Private placement (existing relationships) | Can advertise publicly |
| Most common for CRE funds? | Yes (vast majority) | Growing, especially with online platforms |

### 4.2 Rule 506(b) Requirements

| Requirement | Detail | Action Item |
|-------------|--------|-------------|
| No general solicitation | Cannot advertise the fund publicly (no website, social media, mass email) | Limit outreach to pre-existing substantive relationships |
| Accredited investor standard | Income >$200K (individual) or >$300K (joint) for 2 years; OR net worth >$1M excluding primary residence; OR entity with >$5M in assets; OR "knowledgeable employee" | Collect accreditation representation in subscription agreement |
| Non-accredited investors (max 35) | Must be "sophisticated" (knowledge and experience to evaluate) | Must provide more extensive disclosure (essentially PPM-level) |
| Form D filing | File with SEC within 15 days of first sale of securities | Fund counsel files electronically on EDGAR |
| Blue sky filings | File notice in each state where LPs reside | Fund counsel handles; deadlines vary by state |
| Bad actor disqualification | Verify no covered person has disqualifying events (felony, SEC action, etc.) | Screen all GPs, promoters, compensated solicitors |

### 4.3 Accredited Investor Verification Methods (506(c))

If using Rule 506(c) (general solicitation permitted), the GP must verify accreditation through:

| Method | For Income Test | For Net Worth Test | For Entity Test |
|--------|----------------|-------------------|----------------|
| Tax returns | 2 most recent years showing >$200K/$300K | N/A | N/A |
| W-2s | 2 most recent years | N/A | N/A |
| Written confirmation from CPA, attorney, or broker-dealer | Acceptable for both | Acceptable for both | Acceptable |
| Bank/brokerage statements | N/A | Recent statements showing >$1M (excl. primary residence) | Recent statements showing >$5M assets |
| Third-party verification service | Acceptable | Acceptable | N/A |

### 4.5 Investment Company Act Exemptions

CRE funds must also be exempt from the Investment Company Act of 1940:

| Exemption | Section 3(c)(1) | Section 3(c)(7) |
|-----------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Max investors | 100 beneficial owners | Unlimited |
| Investor qualification | Accredited investors | "Qualified purchasers" ($5M+ investments) |
| Most common for | Emerging managers, smaller funds | Larger institutional funds |
| Practical implication | Limits fund to ~100 LPs | No LP count limit but higher qualification bar |

## Part 5: Emerging Manager Negotiation Guide

### 5.1 Terms LPs Will Push On (and Where to Give)

| Term | LP Request | Market Standard | Give? | Rationale |
|------|-----------|----------------|-------|-----------|
| Management fee | 1.0-1.25% | 1.5% for emerging | Partial -- offer 1.25% for $5M+ commitments | Cannot operate below 1.0% on a small fund |
| GP commitment | 5%+ | 2-3% for emerging | Explain dollar significance; offer to increase if fund exceeds target | |
| Preferred return | 9-10% | 8% | Hold at 8% | 9%+ makes promote nearly unreachable for value-add |
| Carried interest | 15% | 20% | Hold at 20% | Below 20% signals desperation |
| Clawback | Escrowed | Varies | Give 25% escrow | Builds trust at low cost |
| Co-invest | Yes, no-fee | Standard | Give | Increases LP relationship value |
| MFN | Full | For economic terms only | Give for economic terms | Standard accommodation |
| Reporting | Monthly | Quarterly | Offer quarterly; monthly is unsustainable for small teams | |
| Advisory committee seat | Yes | 3-5 seats for largest LPs | Give to top 3-5 LPs | Standard governance |
| No-fault removal | Yes, supermajority | 75% vote | Give at 75% | Standard; lower thresholds are dangerous for GP |

### 5.2 Red Lines for the GP

Do not concede on:
1. **Carry below 20%** -- signals the GP is not investable
2. **Preferred return above 9%** -- mathematically difficult for value-add
3. **Management fee below 1.0% on committed** -- fund cannot cover operating expenses
4. **Key person with no cure period** -- temporary health issue would destroy the fund
5. **LP consent on every investment** -- defeats the purpose of a discretionary fund
6. **Personal guarantee on clawback above 50%** -- disproportionate personal risk
