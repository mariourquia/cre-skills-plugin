# PPM Outline Template: Private Placement Memorandum

---

## TEMPLATE INSTRUCTIONS (strip before use)

This template outlines the sections of a Private Placement Memorandum (PPM) for a CRE fund. The PPM is a legal disclosure document -- not a marketing document. Its primary purpose is to disclose all material information an investor needs to make an informed decision, including all material risks. Failure to disclose material risks exposes the GP to securities fraud liability.

**Who drafts the PPM**: Fund counsel. The GP provides content and reviews for accuracy. The GP should never draft the PPM without counsel involvement.

**This template is**: A content outline to help the GP prepare materials for counsel and understand what the PPM should contain. It is not a substitute for legal advice.

**Typical length**: 60-120 pages depending on fund complexity.

### Section 6: Risk Factors (15-25 pages)

**MOST IMPORTANT SECTION FROM A LEGAL LIABILITY PERSPECTIVE.**

Risk factors must be comprehensive, specific, and not merely boilerplate. Every material risk that could cause an LP to lose money must be disclosed. Failure to disclose is the primary basis for securities fraud claims.

#### Risk Factor Categories

**General Investment Risks**:
- Illiquidity of partnership interests
- No guarantee of return; possibility of total loss
- Limited operating history of the fund (if applicable)
- Reliance on key persons
- Dependence on GP's judgment and discretion
- Forward-looking statements may not be realized

**Real Estate Risks**:
- Real estate values fluctuate and may decline
- Real estate is illiquid
- Environmental contamination liability
- Property damage from natural disaster or casualty
- Uninsured or underinsured losses
- Construction cost overruns and delays
- Changes in local market conditions (vacancy, rent levels, supply)
- Competition from other properties and developers
- Dependence on tenant creditworthiness
- Government regulation (zoning, building codes, rent control)
- ADA compliance requirements
- Eminent domain risk

**Financial and Leverage Risks**:
- Use of leverage amplifies losses as well as gains
- Interest rate increases on floating-rate debt
- Inability to refinance at maturity
- Lender covenants may restrict operations
- Cross-default and cross-collateralization provisions
- Bridge loan maturity default risk
- Credit facility revocation

**Tax Risks**:
- Tax laws may change adversely
- Partnership tax treatment may be challenged by IRS
- State and local tax obligations in multiple jurisdictions
- UBTI implications for tax-exempt investors
- FIRPTA implications for non-US investors
- Carried interest tax treatment uncertainty

**Regulatory Risks**:
- Rent control or stabilization legislation
- Changes in tax assessment methodology (property taxes)
- Environmental regulation changes
- Dodd-Frank implications (if applicable)
- State securities law compliance
- ERISA plan asset rules

**Conflicts of Interest Risks**:
- GP manages multiple vehicles with overlapping strategies
- GP affiliates receive fees from fund assets
- GP has discretion over valuations that affect promote
- GP principals have personal investments in target markets

**Fund Structure Risks**:
- Limited transferability of interests
- No secondary market for fund interests
- Capital calls are binding obligations
- Default penalties are severe
- GP has broad discretionary authority
- Fund term extensions may delay return of capital
