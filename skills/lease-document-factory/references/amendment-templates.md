# Lease Amendment Templates

## Purpose

Standard lease amendment templates for common scenarios encountered in institutional CRE asset management. Each template includes standard provisions, negotiation considerations, and landlord protection clauses.

## Template Structure

Every amendment follows this structure:

```
FIRST [SECOND/THIRD/etc.] AMENDMENT TO LEASE

THIS FIRST AMENDMENT TO LEASE ("Amendment") is made as of [DATE] ("Amendment
Date"), by and between [LANDLORD ENTITY] ("Landlord") and [TENANT ENTITY]
("Tenant").

RECITALS

A. Landlord and Tenant entered into that certain Lease Agreement dated [DATE]
   (the "Original Lease"), as amended by [list all prior amendments, if any]
   (collectively, the "Lease"), for premises located at [ADDRESS], consisting
   of approximately [SF] rentable square feet (the "Premises") in the building
   known as [BUILDING NAME] (the "Building").

B. Landlord and Tenant desire to amend the Lease to [brief description of
   amendment purpose].

AGREEMENT

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants herein and other
good and valuable consideration, the receipt and sufficiency of which are
hereby acknowledged, Landlord and Tenant agree as follows:

[NUMBERED PROVISIONS]

[STANDARD CLOSING PROVISIONS]
```

## Template 1: Rent Restructuring (Blend-and-Extend)

### Scenario
Tenant requests near-term rent reduction in exchange for extended lease term. Common when tenant is facing financial pressure or exploring relocation.

### Standard Provisions

```
1. EXTENDED TERM. The Lease Term is hereby extended for an additional period
   of [X] years (the "Extension Period"), commencing on [DATE] and expiring
   on [DATE] (the "Extended Expiration Date").

2. BASE RENT. Effective as of [DATE], the Base Rent schedule shall be as
   follows:

   Period                    Annual Base Rent    Monthly Base Rent    Rent/SF
   [Current through Orig     $XXX,XXX           $XX,XXX              $XX.XX
    Expiration]
   [Year 1 Extension]        $XXX,XXX           $XX,XXX              $XX.XX
   [Year 2 Extension]        $XXX,XXX           $XX,XXX              $XX.XX
   [etc.]

3. TENANT IMPROVEMENTS. [If applicable] Landlord shall provide a tenant
   improvement allowance of $XX.XX per rentable square foot (the "TI
   Allowance"), not to exceed $XXX,XXX in the aggregate, to be applied
   toward [scope of work].

4. RENT ABATEMENT. [If applicable] Tenant shall receive abatement of Base
   Rent for the period from [DATE] through [DATE] (the "Abatement Period"),
   totaling $XXX,XXX. Tenant shall remain obligated for all Additional Rent
   during the Abatement Period. In the event of Tenant default, the unamortized
   abated rent shall become immediately due and payable.

5. SECURITY DEPOSIT. The Security Deposit shall be [increased/maintained] at
   $XXX,XXX.

6. RENEWAL OPTIONS. [Modify/add/remove renewal options for extended term]

7. GUARANTY. [If applicable] The Guaranty of [GUARANTOR NAME] dated [DATE]
   shall remain in full force and effect and shall apply to all obligations
   under the Lease, as amended hereby.
```

### Negotiation Considerations

| Issue | Landlord Position | Tenant Counter | Resolution Range |
|-------|------------------|----------------|-----------------|
| Blended rate | At or above market for extension period | Flat rate across blend | Near-term discount, step-ups to market by year 3 of extension |
| TI allowance | $0 (tenant already built out) | $10-20/SF for refresh | $5-10/SF, amortized into rent at 8% |
| Free rent | 0 months | 3-6 months | 1-2 months, amortized |
| Extension length | 7-10 years | 3-5 years | 5-7 years minimum to justify blending |
| Guaranty | Maintained through extended term | Released | Maintained, burn-down after 5 years |
| Early termination | None | Midterm kick-out | Kick-out at month 60 with 12-month penalty |

### Landlord Protection Clauses

1. **Recapture of abated rent**: If tenant defaults during the extended term, all abated rent becomes immediately due as liquidated damages.
2. **Reaffirmation**: "Except as expressly modified hereby, the Lease and all of its terms, covenants, and conditions shall remain in full force and effect."
3. **Estoppel representation**: "Tenant hereby confirms that as of the Amendment Date, (a) the Lease is in full force and effect, (b) Landlord is not in default under the Lease, and (c) Tenant has no claims, defenses, or offsets against Landlord."
4. **No guarantor release**: Guarantor must execute a consent and reaffirmation attached as an exhibit.

### Worked Example: Blend-and-Extend

**Scenario**: Office tenant, 3 years remaining on lease, 20,000 SF at $30/SF. Market rent is $34/SF. Tenant requests 5-year extension at blended rate.

**Current position**:
- Remaining rent: 3 years x $30/SF x 20,000 SF = $1,800,000
- NPV at 7%: $1,574,000

**Tenant's ask**: Blend to $28/SF for 8 years (3 remaining + 5 extension)
- Total rent: 8 years x $28/SF x 20,000 SF = $4,480,000
- NPV at 7%: $3,452,000

**Landlord counter**: $26/SF years 1-2, $30/SF year 3, $34/SF years 4-8
- Total rent: blended schedule = $4,920,000
- NPV at 7%: $3,656,000

**Re-leasing alternative**: If tenant vacates in 3 years
- 9 months downtime, $40/SF TI, 6% commission on 5-year deal at $34/SF
- Re-leasing NPV: $3,180,000 (after vacancy, TI, commission costs)

**Recommendation**: Landlord counter produces $474K more NPV than re-leasing. Accept counter at $26/$30/$34 schedule. WALT extends from 3.0 to 8.0 years. Valuation impact at 7% cap: +$650K.

## Template 2: Expansion

### Standard Provisions

```
1. EXPANSION PREMISES. Effective as of [DATE] (the "Expansion Date"), the
   Premises shall be expanded to include approximately [SF] rentable square
   feet located on [FLOOR] of the Building, as shown on Exhibit A attached
   hereto (the "Expansion Premises"). As of the Expansion Date, the total
   Premises shall consist of approximately [TOTAL SF] rentable square feet.

2. EXPANSION PREMISES BASE RENT. Commencing on the Expansion Date, Tenant
   shall pay Base Rent for the Expansion Premises as follows:
   [Rent schedule for expansion space]

3. EXISTING PREMISES RENT. Base Rent for the Original Premises shall
   continue per the existing Lease schedule and shall not be affected by
   this Amendment.

4. TENANT'S PROPORTIONATE SHARE. Tenant's Proportionate Share shall be
   increased from [X]% to [Y]% as of the Expansion Date.

5. TENANT IMPROVEMENTS. Landlord shall provide a TI Allowance of $XX.XX
   per rentable square foot of the Expansion Premises for improvements
   per Exhibit B (Work Letter).

6. PARKING. Tenant's parking allocation shall be increased by [X] spaces,
   from [current] to [new total].

7. SIGNAGE. [If applicable, update signage rights]

8. TERM. The term for the Expansion Premises shall be coterminous with
   the term for the Original Premises, expiring on [DATE].
```

### Key Negotiation Points

- **Coterminous requirement**: Landlord should insist expansion term is coterminous with original lease. If original lease has short remaining term, require extension.
- **Expansion rent**: Should be at market rent, not blended with below-market existing rent. If tenant has expansion option, strike price governs.
- **TI allowance**: Market-rate TI for expansion space. Existing space is irrelevant.
- **Proportionate share recalculation**: Verify the math. Expansion changes the denominator if building SF changes, or only the numerator if building SF is fixed.

## Template 3: Contraction (Give-Back)

### Standard Provisions

```
1. CONTRACTION. Effective as of [DATE] (the "Contraction Date"), the Premises
   shall be reduced by approximately [SF] rentable square feet, as shown on
   Exhibit A (the "Give-Back Space"). As of the Contraction Date, the Premises
   shall consist of approximately [REMAINING SF] rentable square feet.

2. CONTRACTION FEE. In consideration of this Amendment, Tenant shall pay
   Landlord a contraction fee of $XXX,XXX (the "Contraction Fee"), payable
   as follows: [lump sum / installments / amortized into rent].

3. BASE RENT. From and after the Contraction Date, Base Rent shall be
   calculated on the reduced Premises of [SF] rentable square feet at the
   then-applicable per-SF rate under the Lease.

4. PROPORTIONATE SHARE. Tenant's Proportionate Share shall be reduced from
   [X]% to [Y]% as of the Contraction Date.

5. CONDITION OF GIVE-BACK SPACE. Tenant shall deliver the Give-Back Space
   to Landlord in broom-clean condition, with all of Tenant's personal
   property removed, all Tenant cabling removed [or left in place, at
   Landlord's election], and the space in compliance with all applicable
   codes.

6. EXTENDED TERM. In further consideration, the Lease Term for the reduced
   Premises shall be extended through [DATE].
```

### Contraction Fee Calculation

The contraction fee should make the landlord whole for:

| Component | Calculation | Typical Amount |
|-----------|-------------|----------------|
| Unamortized TI | Original TI x (remaining months / original term months) | Varies |
| Unamortized leasing commission | Commission x (remaining months / original term months) | Varies |
| Vacancy/downtime cost | Estimated months to re-lease x give-back SF x market rent / 12 | 6-12 months |
| New leasing commission | Market commission rate x estimated new lease term x market rent x give-back SF | 4-6% Yr 1 |
| New TI for give-back space | Market TI allowance x give-back SF | $20-50/SF office |
| **Total Contraction Fee** | **Sum of above** | **6-18 months rent** |

## Template 4: Use Change

### Standard Provisions

```
1. PERMITTED USE. Section [X] of the Lease is hereby amended to read:
   "Tenant shall use the Premises for [NEW USE DESCRIPTION] and for no
   other purpose without Landlord's prior written consent."

2. COMPLIANCE. Tenant shall, at Tenant's sole cost, comply with all
   applicable laws, codes, and regulations relating to the new use,
   including but not limited to obtaining all necessary permits, licenses,
   and governmental approvals.

3. LANDLORD REPRESENTATIONS. Landlord represents that the new use is
   permitted under the current zoning and building code [OR: Tenant
   acknowledges that Landlord makes no representation regarding zoning
   compliance for the new use].

4. INSURANCE. Tenant's insurance requirements under Section [X] shall be
   modified as follows: [updated coverage requirements appropriate to new
   use, if any].

5. EXCLUSIVE USE. This Amendment shall not be construed to grant Tenant
   any exclusive use right, and Landlord's consent to the use change
   shall not limit Landlord's rights with respect to other tenants in
   the Building.
```

### Pre-Approval Checklist

Before consenting to a use change:
- [ ] Check all other leases for exclusive use conflicts
- [ ] Verify new use is permitted under zoning
- [ ] Verify new use complies with CC&Rs and ground lease (if applicable)
- [ ] Assess impact on building insurance premiums
- [ ] Assess impact on neighboring tenants (noise, traffic, hours, odors)
- [ ] Verify new use does not trigger building code upgrades
- [ ] Review parking requirements for new use
- [ ] Assess impact on building class and reputation

## Template 5: Assignment/Subletting Consent

### Standard Provisions

```
1. CONSENT. Landlord hereby consents to the [assignment of the Lease /
   subletting of approximately [SF] rentable square feet of the Premises]
   to [ASSIGNEE/SUBTENANT NAME] (the "Transferee"), upon the terms set
   forth herein and in the [Assignment Agreement / Sublease] attached as
   Exhibit A.

2. CONDITIONS. This consent is subject to the following conditions:
   (a) The Transferee shall assume [all / applicable] obligations of
       Tenant under the Lease [for assignment] / The Sublease shall be
       subject and subordinate to the Lease [for subletting];
   (b) Tenant shall not be released from any obligation under the Lease;
   (c) The Transferee shall deliver to Landlord certificates of insurance
       meeting the requirements of Section [X] of the Lease;
   (d) This consent shall not be deemed consent to any further transfer.

3. PROFIT SHARING. [If lease provides] Any sublease profit (excess of
   sublease rent over Lease rent, after deduction of reasonable brokerage
   commissions and tenant improvement costs) shall be shared [50/50 or
   per lease terms] between Landlord and Tenant.

4. RECAPTURE. [If applicable] In lieu of this consent, Landlord had the
   right to recapture the [assigned/sublet] space pursuant to Section [X]
   of the Lease. Landlord hereby waives its recapture right with respect
   to this specific transaction only.
```

### Landlord Evaluation Criteria

Before consenting:
1. **Financial strength of transferee**: Equal to or better than current tenant
2. **Use compatibility**: Transferee's use consistent with building and co-tenancy
3. **Lease compliance**: Tenant in good standing, no outstanding defaults
4. **Market position**: Does the sublease undercut landlord's direct leasing efforts?
5. **Profit participation**: Ensure lease profit-sharing clause is enforced

## Template 6: COVID-Era Rent Deferral Repayment

### Standard Provisions

```
1. DEFERRED RENT. Landlord and Tenant acknowledge that Tenant deferred
   Base Rent payments totaling $XXX,XXX for the period from [START DATE]
   through [END DATE] (the "Deferral Period") pursuant to [prior agreement/
   letter dated DATE].

2. REPAYMENT. Tenant shall repay the Deferred Rent in [X] equal monthly
   installments of $XX,XXX, commencing on [DATE] and continuing on the
   first day of each month thereafter until fully repaid, in addition to
   all currently due Base Rent and Additional Rent.

3. INTEREST. The Deferred Rent shall bear interest at [X]% per annum from
   the original due date until repaid [OR: no interest shall accrue].

4. DEFAULT. Failure to make any Deferred Rent repayment when due shall
   constitute a default under the Lease, subject to any applicable notice
   and cure periods.

5. CONFIRMATION OF OBLIGATIONS. Tenant confirms that all rent and other
   charges under the Lease, other than as specifically deferred herein,
   were and remain due and payable in accordance with the Lease terms.

6. NO PRECEDENT. This deferral arrangement shall not establish any precedent
   or create any expectation of future rent relief.
```
