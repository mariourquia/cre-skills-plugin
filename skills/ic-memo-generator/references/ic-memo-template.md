## TEMPLATE INSTRUCTIONS (strip before use)

This template produces a complete Investment Committee memorandum for a single-asset CRE acquisition. Instructions appear in [BRACKETS]. Remove all bracketed instructions before distributing.

**Tone**: Direct, quantitative, no hedging language. IC members are sophisticated investors. Avoid: "we believe", "it is our view that", "we feel". Use: data citations, stated assumptions, explicit qualifications when uncertainty is genuine.

**Length target**: 3-4 pages standard deal. 5-6 pages for value-add or complex structures. Do not pad; do not cut essential analysis.

**Required before distributing**: All financial tables independently verified against underwriting model. No placeholder numbers.

# WORKED EXAMPLE: 50-Unit Multifamily -- Ironbound District, Newark, NJ

---

# INVESTMENT COMMITTEE MEMORANDUM

**Date**: March 2025
**Prepared by**: [Analyst]
**Deal Name**: 142 Ferry Street, Newark, NJ (Ironbound Portfolio)
**Asset Type**: Multifamily (50 Units)
**Market**: Newark, NJ -- Ironbound District
**Stage**: Full Underwriting / IC Approval Request
**Recommendation**: GO WITH CONDITIONS

---

## 1. Executive Summary

- **Acquisition thesis**: Acquiring a 50-unit 1970s-vintage multifamily at a 3.94% going-in cap and $240,000/unit basis, representing an $80,000/unit discount to estimated replacement cost of $320,000/unit in the supply-constrained Ironbound District of Newark, NJ.
- **Value creation mechanism**: Renovating 20 of 50 units at $18,000/unit to capture a $450/month rent premium, driving NOI from $473,000 to $665,000 at stabilization (Year 3), a 40% uplift.
- **Return profile**: 5-year levered IRR of 12.1% and 1.83x equity multiple on $4.74M equity; returns are primarily NOI-growth-driven with modest cap compression contribution.

---

## 2. Deal Overview

### 2.1 Property Summary

| Field | Detail |

| Asset Type | Multifamily |
| Location | 142 Ferry Street, Newark, NJ 07105 |
| Submarket | Ironbound District |
| Year Built / Last Renovated | 1974 / 2012 (partial) |
| Size | 50 units (30 x 1BR, 15 x 2BR, 5 x 3BR) |
| Purchase Price | $12,000,000 ($240,000/unit) |
| Replacement Cost Comparison | $320,000/unit -- 25% discount to replacement |
| Current Occupancy | 94% (3 units vacant) |
| In-Place NOI | $473,000 |
| Ownership / Seller | Private individual (estate sale) |
| Deal Source | Off-market via local broker relationship |
| Closing Date Target | June 2025 |

### 2.2 Investment Highlights

- **Basis protection**: $240K/unit is $80K below replacement cost of $290-330K/unit for new multifamily in Ironbound. Discount makes ground-up development uneconomic at this rental tier, limiting direct competitive supply.
- **Rent spread**: In-place rents average $1,450/month, 23% below the $1,900/month achieved by renovated comparables at 450 Hamilton St. and 280 Ferry St. (December 2024 lease data).
- **Supply constraint**: Ironbound vacancy 3.1% per Q4 2024 CoStar. Zero new deliveries within 0.5 miles in 2024; one pipeline project (48 units, 1.2 miles) with 2027 delivery.
- **IO alignment**: 2-year interest-only period matches renovation and lease-up schedule, minimizing Year 1-2 cash drag.

### 2.3 Deal Risks (Summary)

Renovation timeline slippage (6-month delay = ~115bps IRR reduction) and rent growth underperformance (1% vs. 3% assumed = ~150bps reduction) are the two material risks. Simultaneous occurrence pushes levered IRR to approximately 8.3%.

### 3.3 Sensitivity Summary

At base case (5.50% exit cap, 3.0% rent growth), levered IRR is 12.1%. Deal clears 10.0% hurdle down to 6.00% exit cap with 2.0% rent growth (9.6%). Fails hurdle only at 6.00%+ exit cap with rent growth at or near 0% -- a severe simultaneous deterioration scenario with a low base rate for supply-constrained infill multifamily. 17 of 25 sensitivity cells meet the hurdle.
