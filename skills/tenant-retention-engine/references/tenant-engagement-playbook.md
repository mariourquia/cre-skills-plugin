# Tenant Engagement Playbook

Reference guide for designing, budgeting, executing, and measuring tenant engagement programs in commercial real estate.

## ROI Model

### Turnover Cost Components

| Component | Office (per SF) | Multifamily (per unit) | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Vacancy loss | Varies (months vacant x rent) | $1,500-3,000 (1-2 months) | Primary cost driver |
| Tenant improvements | $15-60/SF (new), $5-15/SF (renewal) | $1,000-5,000 | New TI far exceeds renewal TI |
| Leasing commissions | 4-6% of lease value | 50-100% of one month rent | Paid to brokers |
| Make-ready / turnover maintenance | $1-5/SF | $500-2,500 | Paint, carpet, clean, repairs |
| Marketing/advertising | $2,000-10,000 per vacancy | $200-500/unit/month | Vacancy marketing cost |
| Administrative processing | $500-2,000 | $200-500 | Staff time for lease-up |

### Worked Example: 200-Unit Multifamily

**Baseline scenario (no engagement program)**
- 200 units, $1,800/month average rent
- Annual turnover: 45% = 90 units turning over
- Average turnover cost per unit: $3,000 (1.5 months vacancy at $1,800 + $300 make-ready)
- Total annual turnover cost: 90 x $3,000 = $270,000

**With engagement program**
- Annual engagement budget: $15,000 ($75/unit)
- Program reduces turnover from 45% to 38% = 76 units turning over
- Turnovers avoided: 14 units
- Turnover cost savings: 14 x $3,000 = $42,000
- Net benefit: $42,000 - $15,000 = $27,000
- ROI: $27,000 / $15,000 = 1.8x (or 180%)
- Payback: immediate (savings exceed cost in Year 1)

### Worked Example: 150,000 SF Class B Office

**Baseline scenario**
- 150,000 SF, 12 tenants, $28/SF average rent
- Annual tenant loss rate: 20% by SF = 30,000 SF
- Average backfill cost per SF: $45 (TI $25, commissions $12, vacancy loss $8)
- Total annual turnover cost: 30,000 x $45 = $1,350,000

**With engagement program**
- Annual engagement budget: $75,000 ($0.50/SF x 150,000)
- Program reduces loss rate from 20% to 15% = 22,500 SF lost
- SF retained: 7,500 SF
- Turnover cost savings: 7,500 x $45 = $337,500
- Net benefit: $337,500 - $75,000 = $262,500
- ROI: $262,500 / $75,000 = 3.5x (or 350%)

### Break-Even Formula

Break-even point = Annual engagement budget / Turnover cost per unit (or per SF)

If engagement budget = $50,000 and each prevented turnover saves $25,000:
Break-even = 2 prevented turnovers per year.

If your building has 20 tenants with 25% historical turnover (5 turnovers/year), you need to prevent 2 out of 5 turnovers -- a 40% effectiveness rate on at-risk tenants.

## Measurement Framework

### Leading Indicators (measure monthly/quarterly)

| Metric | Measurement Method | Target |
|---|---|---|
| Event attendance rate | Attendees / invited tenants | >40% for signature events, >20% for recurring |
| Event satisfaction | Post-event survey (1-5 scale) | >4.0 average |
| Tenant interaction frequency | Management touchpoints per tenant per quarter | >3 meaningful interactions |
| Survey response rate | Annual survey responses / total tenants | >50% |
| Work order satisfaction | Post-resolution rating | >4.0 average |

### Lagging Indicators (measure annually)

| Metric | Measurement Method | Target |
|---|---|---|
| Tenant retention rate | Renewed SF / expiring SF | Improve 3-7 percentage points year-over-year |
| NPS score | Annual tenant survey | Improve 5-10 points year-over-year |
| Overall satisfaction score | Annual survey composite | >4.0 (on 5-point scale) |
| Referral rate | New tenants referred by existing tenants | >10% of new leases |
| Online reviews (MF) | Google/Yelp/ApartmentRatings | >4.0 stars, improving trend |

### Correlation Analysis

Track these relationships to quantify engagement impact:

1. **Attendance to renewal**: tenants attending 3+ events/year renew at 15-25% higher rate than non-attendees. Track attendance by tenant and compare to renewal outcomes.
2. **Satisfaction to retention**: each 0.5-point improvement in overall satisfaction score correlates with 5-8% improvement in retention rate.
3. **Management interaction to satisfaction**: tenants reporting 4+ meaningful interactions per quarter score 0.8-1.2 points higher on satisfaction surveys.
4. **Response time to satisfaction**: buildings with <4-hour work order acknowledgment score 10-15% higher on maintenance satisfaction.

### Annual Engagement Program Review

Conduct annually (December/January) covering:

1. Total events held vs. planned
2. Total spend vs. budget (by category)
3. Average attendance rates and trends
4. Satisfaction scores and trends
5. Retention rate change (year-over-year)
6. ROI calculation (savings from improved retention vs. program cost)
7. Tenant feedback themes (what they want more/less of)
8. Next-year program design recommendations
9. Budget request for upcoming year (justified by ROI data)

## Event Execution Checklist

### 8 Weeks Before
- [ ] Set event date, time, and location
- [ ] Define budget and get approval
- [ ] Book venue/space (if external) or reserve common area
- [ ] Identify vendors (catering, entertainment, rentals)

### 4 Weeks Before
- [ ] Confirm all vendor bookings
- [ ] Design invitation/communications
- [ ] Send save-the-date to all tenants
- [ ] Arrange any permits or insurance (if needed)

### 2 Weeks Before
- [ ] Send formal invitation with RSVP
- [ ] Confirm headcount estimates with caterer
- [ ] Plan setup logistics (tables, signage, AV)
- [ ] Assign staff roles (greeting, setup, cleanup)

### 1 Week Before
- [ ] Final headcount to vendors
- [ ] Confirm delivery/setup times
- [ ] Prepare signage and materials
- [ ] Send reminder to tenants

### Day Of
- [ ] Setup 2 hours before event
- [ ] Photographer on-site
- [ ] Management team visible and engaging
- [ ] Collect feedback (informal or brief survey)
- [ ] Document attendance

### 1 Week After
- [ ] Send thank-you email with photos
- [ ] Compile attendance and feedback data
- [ ] Process vendor invoices
- [ ] Update engagement tracking spreadsheet
- [ ] Note improvements for next event
