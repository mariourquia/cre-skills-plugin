# Competitive Intelligence Template: Tenant BATNA Mapping and Competitor Space Analysis

---

## Purpose

This template provides the framework for understanding a tenant's alternatives before entering renewal negotiations. Knowing the tenant's Best Alternative to a Negotiated Agreement (BATNA) is the single most important input into a retention negotiation. If you do not know the tenant's alternatives, you are negotiating blind.

**Rule**: Never enter a renewal negotiation without completing at least the BATNA summary (Part 1) and the competitor space survey (Part 2). This takes 2-4 hours of research per tenant and saves tens of thousands in unnecessary concessions.

---

## Part 1: Tenant BATNA Mapping

### 1.1 Framework

A tenant's BATNA consists of four components:

| Component | Definition | How to Assess |
|-----------|-----------|---------------|
| **Alternative spaces** | Other properties the tenant could move to | Competitor space survey (Part 2) |
| **Moving costs** | Total cost of relocation (physical move, TI, downtime, employee disruption) | Moving cost calculator (Section 1.3) |
| **Business disruption** | Operational impact of moving (client access, employee commute, brand identity) | Qualitative assessment |
| **Switching willingness** | How motivated is the tenant to explore alternatives? | Tenant relationship intelligence (Section 1.4) |

### 1.4 Tenant Relationship Intelligence

Gather these data points through regular tenant communication (not just at renewal):

| Intelligence Item | How to Obtain | Significance |
|------------------|---------------|-------------|
| Headcount trend | Ask directly; observe parking utilization | Growing = needs more space; shrinking = may downsize |
| Client/customer location | Ask or observe foot traffic | If clients visit, location matters more |
| Employee commute patterns | Informal conversation | If employees live nearby, moving is harder |
| Business performance | Public data (SEC filings, news); private (ask) | Struggling business = flight risk; growing = opportunity |
| Lease satisfaction | Maintenance response, common area quality | Unhappy tenant explores earlier |
| Decision-maker identity | Direct relationship | Know who signs the check and who influences |
| Budget cycle | Ask | Time renewal to align with tenant's fiscal year |
| Competing proposals | Broker intel; ask directly | "Have you been shown any other spaces?" |

### 2.2 Competitive Positioning Matrix

Plot your property vs. competitors on a 2x2 matrix:

```
                        HIGHER QUALITY
                            |
              Quadrant 2    |    Quadrant 1
              (New/renovated|    (Premium/trophy)
              at premium    |
              rent)         |
                            |
    LOWER RENT -------------|-------------- HIGHER RENT
                            |
              Quadrant 3    |    Quadrant 4
              (Value play:  |    (Overpriced for
              good space,   |    quality -- vulnerable)
              below-market) |
                            |
                        LOWER QUALITY
```

**Your position**: Determine which quadrant your property occupies relative to the tenant's alternatives. This determines your negotiation strategy:

| Quadrant | Strategy |
|----------|---------|
| Q1 (premium quality, premium rent) | Justify the premium with specific amenity/location value |
| Q2 (high quality, lower rent) | Your strongest position -- tenant has no better option at this price |
| Q3 (value play) | Competitive on price; must demonstrate reliability and service |
| Q4 (overpriced for quality) | Vulnerable. Must reduce rent, increase TI, or improve space to retain |

### 2.3 Worked Example: Competitor Survey for 20,000 SF Law Firm

| Property | Class | Avail SF | Asking | TI | Free Rent | Effective | Distance | Notes |
|----------|-------|----------|--------|-----|-----------|-----------|----------|-------|
| Subject (ours) | B+ | N/A | $32/SF | $10 | 1 mo | $30.10 | -- | Renewal scenario |
| 100 Main St | A | 22,000 | $38/SF | $45 | 3 mo | $30.85 | 0.8 mi | New construction |
| 200 Broad St | B | 18,000 | $30/SF | $15 | 2 mo | $27.50 | 1.2 mi | Renovated 2023 |
| 300 Park Ave | A- | 25,000 | $35/SF | $35 | 2 mo | $29.20 | 2.0 mi | Different submarket |
| 400 River Rd | B- | 30,000 | $26/SF | $10 | 1 mo | $24.30 | 3.5 mi | Older; suburban |

**Analysis**:

The tenant's best alternative is 300 Park Ave at an effective rent of $29.20/SF -- $0.90/SF below our renewal offer of $30.10/SF. However, 300 Park Ave is 2.0 miles from the courthouse (the law firm's primary destination). The firm's 12 attorneys collectively make 800+ courthouse trips per year. At $0.90/SF savings on 20,000 SF = $18,000/year, the annual savings do not justify the commute disruption.

200 Broad St at $27.50/SF effective is the value alternative. It is $2.60/SF below our offer but requires a 1.2 mile move from the courthouse. The space is 2,000 SF smaller than the firm's current footprint, which would require subletting or reducing headcount.

**Negotiation implication**: The tenant's BATNA is moderate. Our effective rent of $30.10/SF is within $1-3/SF of the best alternatives, and none of the alternatives perfectly match the tenant's size and location needs. We have pricing power. A renewal at $32-33/SF with $10/SF TI and 1 month free rent is defensible. We should not offer more than $15/SF TI unless the tenant produces a signed LOI from a competitor.

## Part 3: Competitive Intelligence Sources

### 3.1 Data Sources by Reliability

| Source | Reliability | Cost | What It Provides |
|--------|-----------|------|-----------------|
| CoStar / LoopNet | High for availability; medium for rent | $10-30K/yr | Vacancy, asking rents, comps, building data |
| Broker intel (friendly brokers) | Medium-High | Free (relationship) | Real deal terms, TI, effective rents, tenant tours |
| Property website / listing flyers | Medium | Free | Asking rents, availability, amenities |
| Direct inquiry (call as prospect) | High | Free | Actual offering terms |
| County assessor records | High for ownership, taxes | Free | Tax assessment, ownership, sale history |
| SEC filings (public tenants) | High | Free | Lease obligations, expansion plans, subleases |
| LinkedIn / news | Low-Medium | Free | Headcount changes, expansion/contraction signals |
| Tour the space yourself | High | Time cost | Actual condition, buildout quality, parking, feel |

### 3.2 Quarterly Monitoring Checklist

Do not wait for lease expiration to gather intelligence. Monitor quarterly:

| Item | Frequency | Action |
|------|-----------|--------|
| Submarket vacancy rate and trend | Quarterly | CoStar pull |
| New space delivered or under construction | Quarterly | CoStar pipeline report |
| Asking rents at top 5 competitor properties | Quarterly | Broker check-in or direct inquiry |
| Tenant headcount and space utilization | Quarterly | Observation + informal conversation |
| Tenant financial health (public tenants) | Quarterly | SEC filings, news alerts |
| Sublease inventory in submarket | Quarterly | CoStar; sublease availability competes with direct |

## Part 4: Negotiation Strategy by BATNA Strength

### Weak BATNA (Tenant Has Few Good Alternatives)

| Tactic | Description |
|--------|------------|
| Anchor high | Start at 5-8% above current rent; negotiate to 3-5% increase |
| Minimal concessions | TI only for demonstrable needs (worn carpet, dated buildout) |
| Push for longer term | 7-10 year renewal locks in revenue and extends WALT |
| Early renewal incentive | "Sign 6 months early and we waive the first month of TI construction" |

### Moderate BATNA (Tenant Has Some Options)

| Tactic | Description |
|--------|------------|
| Match effective rent | Calculate competitor's effective rent; match or beat by $0.50-1.00/SF |
| Targeted TI | Offer TI for specific improvements the tenant values (new conference room, kitchen) |
| Flexible term | Offer 5-year with option to renew for 5 more at a predetermined escalation |
| Highlight switching costs | Present moving cost analysis to tenant (they may not have calculated it) |

### Strong BATNA (Tenant Has Good Alternatives and Is Motivated)

| Tactic | Description |
|--------|------------|
| Lead with value, not price | Emphasize location, service, relationship before discussing rent |
| Compete on TI | Match or exceed competitor TI; this is the primary lever for office tenants |
| Free rent concession | 1-3 months free rent reduces effective rent without lowering face rent |
| Building improvements | Offer to upgrade common areas, add amenities, improve parking |
| Creative structures | Blend and extend (start new term now at blended rate); expansion rights; contraction rights |
| NPV check | Run the retention NPV model. If NPV is negative, let the tenant go gracefully |
