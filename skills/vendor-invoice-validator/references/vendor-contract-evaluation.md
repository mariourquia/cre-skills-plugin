# Vendor Contract Evaluation Reference Guide

Benchmarks, decision frameworks, and templates for evaluating specialty vendor contracts in commercial real estate.

## Specialty Vendor Contract Benchmarks

### Pest Control / IPM

| Property Type | Rate Range ($/SF/month) | Annual ($/SF) | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Office (standard) | $0.02-0.05 | $0.24-0.60 | Monthly service, quarterly deep inspection |
| Office (food service on-site) | $0.04-0.07 | $0.48-0.84 | Bi-weekly service, monthly deep inspection |
| Retail (general) | $0.03-0.06 | $0.36-0.72 | Monthly perimeter + interior |
| Retail (food/grocery) | $0.06-0.10 | $0.72-1.20 | Weekly service, daily monitoring |
| Multifamily | $0.03-0.06 | $0.36-0.72 | Monthly common area + unit rotation |
| Industrial/warehouse | $0.01-0.03 | $0.12-0.36 | Quarterly perimeter + rodent program |

IPM premium: comprehensive IPM programs cost 10-20% more than reactive-only pest control but reduce long-term treatment costs by 30-50% and minimize chemical exposure liability.

### Elevator Maintenance

| Service Level | Rate ($/unit/month) | Includes | Excludes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Oil-and-grease only | $75-200 | Lubrication, basic adjustments, safety tests | Parts, callbacks, modernization |
| Preventive maintenance | $150-300 | Above + minor parts, callbacks during hours | Major parts (controller, motor, cables) |
| Full maintenance | $250-400 | Above + all parts except obsolete | Vandalism, Acts of God, cab finishes |
| Full maintenance + callbacks | $300-500 | Above + 24/7 callback response | Same as above |

Key contract terms to verify:
- Parts ownership: who owns replaced parts? (owner should retain)
- Response time SLA: 30 minutes to 2 hours for entrapment, 4 hours standard callback
- Overtime callback rates: verify not excluded from full-maintenance contract
- Modernization reserve: $15,000-25,000/unit/year for future modernization planning
- Proprietary lock-in: avoid contracts that install proprietary controllers limiting future vendor choice

## Competitive Rebid Decision Matrix

### Scoring Guide

Score each dimension 1-5:

**Performance (weight: 30%)**
- 5: Consistently exceeds SLA, zero deficiencies on inspections
- 4: Meets SLA, rare deficiencies (<2/quarter), promptly corrected
- 3: Generally meets SLA, occasional deficiencies (3-5/quarter)
- 2: Frequently misses SLA, recurring deficiencies (>5/quarter)
- 1: Chronic underperformance, tenant complaints, code violations

**Responsiveness (weight: 25%)**
- 5: Same-day emergency response, proactive communication, anticipates needs
- 4: Meets emergency SLA, responsive to routine requests within 24 hours
- 3: Generally responsive but occasional delays (48+ hours for routine)
- 2: Slow response, requires multiple follow-ups, poor communication
- 1: Unreachable, missed emergencies, no proactive communication

**Price (weight: 25%)**
- 5: 10%+ below market benchmark, stable rates, transparent billing
- 4: At or slightly below market, reasonable escalations (<3%/year)
- 3: At market, standard escalations
- 2: 10-20% above market, aggressive escalations or frequent change orders
- 1: >20% above market, excessive change orders, billing disputes

**Compliance (weight: 20%)**
- 5: Always current on insurance, licenses, safety training; zero incidents
- 4: Current on all requirements, rare lapses promptly corrected
- 3: Occasional insurance lapses, one minor safety incident
- 2: Repeated insurance lapses, safety concerns, licensing gaps
- 1: Uninsured periods, serious safety violations, regulatory citations

### Decision Thresholds

| Weighted Score | Action | Timeline |
|---|---|---|
| 4.0-5.0 | Extend contract | Negotiate 30-60 days before expiration |
| 3.0-3.9 | Extend with conditions | Issue performance improvement plan, renegotiate rates |
| 2.0-2.9 | Competitive rebid | Issue RFP 120-180 days before expiration |
| 1.0-1.9 | Replace immediately | Terminate for cause if contract permits, otherwise do not renew |

### Switching Cost Analysis

Before rebidding, quantify switching costs:

| Cost Category | Estimate | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Service gap risk | 1-2 weeks disruption | Overlap period mitigates but adds cost |
| Mobilization/demobilization | $2,000-10,000 | Equipment, supplies, uniforms, setup |
| Learning curve | 60-90 days to full performance | New vendor needs to learn building systems |
| Administrative | 20-40 hours staff time | RFP, evaluation, contract negotiation, transition management |
| Institutional knowledge loss | Unquantifiable | Long-tenure vendors know building quirks |

Rule of thumb: switching cost equals roughly 2-4 months of contract value. If the annual savings from a new vendor does not exceed switching cost in Year 1, the rebid may not be economically justified unless performance is the driver.

## IPM Program Evaluation Criteria

### Documentation Requirements (score each 1-5)

1. **IPM Plan**: Written plan covering target pests, monitoring methods, action thresholds, treatment protocols, and prevention strategies
2. **Service Reports**: Each visit documented with areas inspected, pests found (type, count, location), treatments applied (product name, EPA reg #, quantity), and follow-up recommendations
3. **Pesticide Use Log**: Maintained per EPA and state requirements, accessible to property manager on demand
4. **Applicator Credentials**: All technicians hold current state applicator license with appropriate categories
5. **Safety Data Sheets**: Current SDS for all products used, accessible on-site

### Program Effectiveness Metrics

| Metric | Target | Red Flag |
|---|---|---|
| Pest incident trend | Declining or stable-low | Increasing over 3+ months |
| Callback rate | <5% of service visits | >10% indicates ineffective treatment |
| Tenant complaints | <2 per quarter per 100K SF | >5 indicates program failure |
| Health dept findings | Zero pest-related citations | Any citation = immediate review |
| Treatment escalation | <20% of visits require chemical treatment | >50% suggests poor prevention |
| Exclusion recommendations | Implemented within 30 days | Backlog >5 open recommendations |

### Common IPM Program Failures

1. **Chemical-first approach**: Vendor applies pesticides every visit regardless of pest activity. True IPM uses monitoring and thresholds.
2. **Cookie-cutter service**: Same treatment schedule for all properties regardless of actual pest pressure, property type, or season.
3. **Poor documentation**: "Serviced building" is not a service report. Require specifics.
4. **Ignoring exclusion**: Treating symptoms (pests inside) without addressing causes (entry points, sanitation). Vendor should recommend building maintenance repairs.
5. **Overreliance on bait stations**: Exterior bait stations are part of a program, not the entire program. Interior monitoring traps should supplement.
6. **No seasonal adjustment**: Pest pressure varies seasonally. Program should adapt treatment focus (ants/flies in summer, rodents in fall/winter, stored product pests year-round in food areas).
