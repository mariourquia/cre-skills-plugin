# Transition Frameworks

## Purpose

Three distinct transition frameworks for CRE asset management: (1) acquisition onboarding (seller-to-buyer), (2) development-to-operations (construction-to-stabilization), and (3) PM company change (incumbent-to-replacement). Each framework includes timeline, document transfer, communication strategy, and risk management.

### Vendor Transition Decision Framework

For each vendor contract inherited from seller:

```
1. Is the vendor performing well per PM assessment?
   YES -> Proceed to pricing review
   NO -> Issue termination notice per contract; begin RFP

2. Is the vendor's pricing competitive with market?
   YES (within 10% of market) -> Retain
   NO (>10% above market) -> Negotiate or issue RFP

3. Is the contract on auto-renewal?
   YES -> Note cancellation window; decide before deadline
   NO -> Will expire naturally; plan transition

4. Does the contract have above-market terms or restrictions?
   YES -> Negotiate modifications or plan exit at term
   NO -> Retain

Decision: RETAIN / RENEGOTIATE / TERMINATE / REPLACE AT EXPIRATION
```

### Tenant Communication Strategy

**Day 1 Letter**: Sent via mail and email to all tenants

Content:
1. Ownership change announcement (professional, reassuring tone)
2. New management company name and contact information
3. New rent payment instructions (lockbox address, online portal URL, wire instructions)
4. Emergency contact numbers (property, management, after-hours)
5. Assurance that all lease terms remain in effect
6. Request for updated contact information and emergency contacts
7. Estoppel certificate attached (if sending with welcome letter)

**Follow-up communications**:
- Week 2: Email with online payment portal registration instructions
- Week 4: Personal introduction from property manager (site visit or call for top tenants)
- Month 2: Tenant satisfaction survey
- Month 3: Newsletter introducing improvement plans and ownership vision

**Tone**: Professional, calm, forward-looking. Avoid criticizing prior ownership. Emphasize continuity of service with planned improvements.

### Operating Budget Creation from Development Budget

**Step 1**: Extract operating cost assumptions from the development pro forma.

**Step 2**: Validate against actual conditions:

| Line Item | Dev Pro Forma | Market Benchmark | Actual Bid/Quote | Budget |
|-----------|--------------|------------------|-------------------|--------|
| Property taxes | $X/SF | $X/SF | Assessed value x rate | |
| Insurance | $X/SF | $X/SF | Bound premium | |
| Utilities | $X/unit | $X/unit | Rate schedule x usage est | |
| R&M | $X/unit | $X/unit | Vendor contracts + reserve | |
| Contract services | $X/unit | $X/unit | Signed contracts | |
| Payroll | $X/unit | $X/unit | Approved staffing plan | |
| Management fee | X% of EGI | X% of EGI | PM agreement | |
| Marketing | $X/unit | $X/unit | Marketing plan budget | |
| G&A | $X/unit | $X/unit | Estimated | |

**Step 3**: Apply lease-up adjustments:
- Revenue ramps from 0 to stabilized over lease-up period
- Concessions modeled per unit/space on a monthly basis
- Variable expenses (utilities, R&M) scale with occupancy
- Fixed expenses (taxes, insurance, contracts) begin at TCO regardless of occupancy
- Management fee based on collected revenue (lower during lease-up)

## Framework 3: PM Company Change (Incumbent-to-Replacement)

### 90-Day Transition Timeline

| Phase | Duration | Key Activities |
|-------|----------|---------------|
| **Phase 1: Selection** | Day -90 to -60 | Issue RFP, evaluate proposals, select replacement PM |
| **Phase 2: Planning** | Day -60 to -30 | Execute PM agreement, plan transition, notify tenants |
| **Phase 3: Transition** | Day -30 to 0 | Data migration, vendor re-contracting, staff transition |
| **Phase 4: Stabilization** | Day 0 to +30 | Go-live, monitoring, issue resolution |

### Phase 1: PM Selection (Day -90 to -60)

**RFP Distribution**: Send RFP to 3-5 qualified PM firms. Include:
- Property description and current operations overview
- Scope of management services required
- Staffing expectations
- Reporting requirements
- Fee structure expectations
- Timeline for transition

**Evaluation Criteria**:

| Criterion | Weight | Scoring |
|-----------|--------|---------|
| Relevant portfolio experience | 25% | Property type, size, market match |
| Proposed team qualifications | 20% | Resumes, availability, track record |
| Fee competitiveness | 20% | Base fee, incentives, pass-throughs |
| Technology platform | 15% | Accounting, work orders, tenant portal |
| Transition plan quality | 10% | Detail, timeline, risk mitigation |
| References | 10% | Owner references checked |

### Phase 2: Planning (Day -60 to -30)

| # | Activity | Owner | Deadline |
|---|----------|-------|----------|
| 1 | Execute new PM agreement | AM/Counsel | Day -60 |
| 2 | Issue termination notice to incumbent PM (per contract) | AM | Day -60 |
| 3 | Schedule transition kickoff meeting (owner, old PM, new PM) | AM | Day -55 |
| 4 | Develop data migration plan (accounting, lease, vendor data) | New PM/AM | Day -50 |
| 5 | Draft tenant notification letter (ownership retains, PM changes) | AM | Day -45 |
| 6 | Send tenant notification letter | AM | Day -40 |
| 7 | New PM staff site visits and orientation | New PM | Day -35 |
| 8 | Vendor notification and contract assignment/re-execution | New PM/AM | Day -35 |
| 9 | Set up new PM in banking/lockbox systems | Accounting | Day -30 |
| 10 | New PM completes system setup (accounting, work orders, portal) | New PM | Day -30 |

#### Staff Transition

| Scenario | Approach |
|----------|----------|
| New PM hires own staff | Old PM staff is released per old PM agreement; new staff begins Day 0 |
| New PM hires incumbent staff | Negotiate with old PM to release staff early; minimize gap |
| Hybrid | New PM brings key management; hires select incumbent maintenance/admin staff |

**Important**: Ensure no staffing gap. On-site maintenance coverage must be continuous. Overlap old and new PM staff by at least 1 week if possible.

### Risk Mitigation

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|
| Tenant confusion (paying wrong entity) | Multiple notifications, monitor old lockbox for 90 days, auto-forward |
| Data loss during migration | Full backup from old PM before transition, parallel systems during overlap |
| Vendor service interruption | Notify vendors 30 days in advance, confirm service continuity in writing |
| Staffing gap on-site | Overlap old/new PM staff by 1 week minimum |
| Open work orders falling through cracks | Export full work order list, assign to new PM team Day 1 |
| Financial reconciliation disputes | Old PM provides final accounting within 60 days; retain transition escrow if needed |
| Tenant relationship damage | Proactive communication, personal touch from new PM, visible improvements early |
