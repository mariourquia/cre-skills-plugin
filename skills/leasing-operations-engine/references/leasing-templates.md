# Leasing Templates Reference

Inquiry response templates, tour preparation checklists, talking points generator, space sheet template, and competitive positioning one-pager. All examples use a baseline 200,000 SF Class A office building in Midtown Manhattan with 15% vacancy.

---

## 1. Inquiry Response Templates

### Template A: Broker Inquiry

```
Subject: RE: [Property Name] -- [Suite/SF] Availability for [Tenant Name]

[Broker First Name],

Thanks for the inquiry on [Property Name] for [Tenant Name]. Happy to help.

We have [n] options that could work for [tenant's SF requirement]:

  Suite [A]: [SF] RSF, [floor] floor
    Condition: [turn-key / warm shell / second-gen]
    Asking rent: $[X]/RSF + electric
    Available: [immediately / date]

  Suite [B]: [SF] RSF, [floor] floor
    Condition: [turn-key / warm shell / second-gen]
    Asking rent: $[X]/RSF + electric
    Available: [immediately / date]

[If relevant:] We can combine Suites [A] and [B] for a contiguous [total SF]
block if your client needs the full footprint.

Current concession: [X months free on a Y-year term]. Landlord willing to
contribute $[X]/RSF toward TI for a [minimum term]-year lease.

I've attached the space sheets and floor plans. When works best for a tour?
I have availability [Day 1] and [Day 2] -- happy to accommodate your client's
schedule.

Best,
[Leasing Agent Name]
[Title]
[Phone] | [Email]
[Property Name] | [Address]

Attachments:
  - Suite [A] space sheet and floor plan
  - Suite [B] space sheet and floor plan
  - Building brochure
```

**Customization variables**:
- `[Property Name]`: building name
- `[Suite/SF]`: suite numbers and square footage
- `[Tenant Name]`: prospect company (per broker's inquiry)
- `[Broker First Name]`: personal address
- `[n]`: number of matching availabilities
- Concession and TI allowance: per current offering
- Tour dates: offer 2-3 specific options within 48 hours

### Template B: Direct Tenant Inquiry

```
Subject: Thank you for your interest in [Property Name]

Dear [Contact First Name],

Thank you for your interest in [Property Name] at [Address]. I'd love to help
you find the right space for [Company Name].

Based on your requirements ([SF needed] square feet, [move-in timeframe]),
I have [n] spaces that could be a great fit. I've attached the details for
each, including floor plans and current pricing.

A few highlights about [Property Name] that are relevant to your search:

  - [Highlight 1: relevant to prospect's industry or stated needs]
  - [Highlight 2: building amenity or recent improvement]
  - [Highlight 3: location/transportation advantage]

The best way to evaluate the space is a brief tour -- typically 20-30 minutes.
I'm available [Day/Time Option 1] or [Day/Time Option 2]. Which works best
for you?

In the meantime, please don't hesitate to reach out with any questions.

Best regards,
[Leasing Agent Name]
[Title]
[Phone] | [Email]
[Property Name] | [Address]

Attachments:
  - Space availability summary
  - Building amenity overview
  - Location and transportation map
```

**Response timing**: online leads must receive a response within 1 hour during business hours. If volume is high, use auto-acknowledgment with personal follow-up within 4 hours.

## 2. Tour Preparation Checklist

### Pre-Tour Checklist (Complete 2-4 Hours Before Tour)

```
Tour Preparation -- [Property Name]
Prospect: [Company/Name]
Tour Date/Time: [date/time]
Leasing Agent: [name]
Broker (if any): [name/firm]

Space Preparation:
  [ ] Suite(s) unlocked and accessible
  [ ] Lights on and functioning (replace any burned-out bulbs)
  [ ] HVAC set to comfortable temperature (68-72F)
  [ ] Space is clean (floors, windows, surfaces)
  [ ] No odors (check for mold, chemicals, food from adjacent tenants)
  [ ] Restrooms clean and stocked (if showing restrooms in suite)
  [ ] Prior tenant signage removed
  [ ] Construction or maintenance work paused during tour window

Building Preparation:
  [ ] Lobby clean and presentable
  [ ] Elevator programmed (hold car on lobby level at tour time)
  [ ] Parking spot designated for prospect (if applicable)
  [ ] Security notified of visitor (add to visitor list)
  [ ] Amenity spaces available for viewing (conference center, gym, etc.)
  [ ] Any active construction or disruption noted (prepare talking point)

Materials:
  [ ] Space sheet(s) with current pricing printed (3 copies)
  [ ] Floor plan(s) printed (3 copies)
  [ ] Building brochure
  [ ] Business cards
  [ ] Proposal template (if ready to leave behind)
  [ ] Tape measure (prospects often ask)
  [ ] Keys / access cards for all spaces being shown

Intelligence:
  [ ] Review prospect's company (website, recent news, headcount)
  [ ] Review prospect's current space (where, size, lease expiry if known)
  [ ] Identify what competing properties prospect is touring
  [ ] Prepare responses to likely objections
  [ ] Review broker's recent deals (if broker-represented)
  [ ] Check if prospect has toured this building before
```

### Tour Talking Points Generator

**Customize based on prospect profile**:

```
Prospect industry: Technology / Creative
  Emphasize: open floor plans, high ceilings, natural light, fiber/connectivity,
             bike storage, casual atmosphere, nearby dining/retail

Prospect industry: Financial Services / Professional Services
  Emphasize: building prestige/address, security, conference facilities,
             executive finishes, efficient floor plates, on-site management

Prospect industry: Healthcare / Medical
  Emphasize: ADA compliance, HVAC capacity for medical equipment, plumbing
             infrastructure, after-hours access, parking, signage visibility

Prospect priority: Cost-conscious
  Emphasize: value proposition vs. competitors, flexible lease terms,
             TI allowance, operating cost transparency, concession package

Prospect priority: Growth / Expansion
  Emphasize: expansion options (right of first refusal on adjacent space),
             flexible demising, short-term lease options, building capacity

Prospect priority: Sustainability / ESG
  Emphasize: LEED certification, ENERGY STAR score, building recycling program,
             EV charging, transit access, energy efficiency investments
```

## 4. Competitive Positioning One-Pager

### Template

```
COMPETITIVE ANALYSIS -- [Property Name] vs. Market
Date prepared: [date]
Prepared for: [prospect name] (internal use only)

=================================================================

YOUR PROPERTY: [Property Name]
  Asking Rent: $[XX.XX]/RSF [full service]
  Concession:  [X months free on X-year term]
  Effective Rent: $[XX.XX]/RSF (net of concessions)
  TI Allowance: $[XX.XX]/RSF
  Total Occupancy Cost (Year 1): $[XX.XX]/RSF

-----------------------------------------------------------------

COMP 1: [Building Name]
  Address: [address]
  Class: [A/B]
  Available SF: [XX,XXX]
  Asking Rent: $[XX.XX]/RSF
  Concession: [details]
  Effective Rent: $[XX.XX]/RSF
  TI: $[XX.XX]/RSF
  ADVANTAGE: [your property's advantage over this comp]
  WEAKNESS: [where this comp is stronger]

COMP 2: [Building Name]
  [same format]

COMP 3: [Building Name]
  [same format]

-----------------------------------------------------------------

POSITIONING SUMMARY:

  Price position:  [Below market / At market / Above market]
  Value drivers:   [List 3 key differentiators]
  Objection prep:  [Anticipated objections and responses]

  Recommended strategy for [prospect]:
    [1-2 sentences on how to position against the specific comps
     this prospect is likely considering]

=================================================================
```

### Worked Example

```
COMPETITIVE ANALYSIS -- 250 Park Avenue vs. Market
Date prepared: 2026-03-15
Prepared for: TechVenture Inc. (internal use only)

YOUR PROPERTY: 250 Park Avenue
  Asking Rent: $72.00/RSF (full service)
  Concession: 6 months free on 7-year term
  Effective Rent: $61.71/RSF
  TI Allowance: $75.00/RSF
  Total Occupancy Cost (Year 1): $61.71/RSF

COMP 1: 300 Madison Avenue
  Class A, 15,000 SF available on 22nd floor
  Asking: $76.00/RSF
  Concession: 4 months free on 7-year
  Effective: $67.71/RSF
  TI: $65.00/RSF
  ADVANTAGE: We're $6.00/RSF cheaper effective and offer $10 more in TI.
  WEAKNESS: 300 Madison has better retail amenities at ground level.

COMP 2: 1 Vanderbilt Avenue
  Class A+, 8,200 SF sublease on 40th floor
  Asking: $95.00/RSF (sublease)
  Concession: none (sublease as-is)
  Effective: $95.00/RSF
  TI: $0 (furnished sublease)
  ADVANTAGE: We're $33/RSF cheaper and offer a direct lease with TI.
  WEAKNESS: 1 Vanderbilt has trophy cachet and pre-built space.

COMP 3: 245 Park Avenue
  Class A, 12,000 SF on 18th floor
  Asking: $70.00/RSF
  Concession: 8 months free on 10-year
  Effective: $56.67/RSF
  TI: $80.00/RSF
  ADVANTAGE: We offer a shorter commitment (7 vs 10 years) -- critical
             for a growth-stage tech company that may scale in 3-5 years.
  WEAKNESS: 245 Park is $5/RSF cheaper effective and higher TI (but
             requires a 10-year commitment).

POSITIONING SUMMARY:
  Price position: At market
  Value drivers:  Flexible term (7-year vs 10), high TI ($75/RSF), recent
                  lobby renovation (2024), on-site conference center
  Objection prep: "245 Park is cheaper" -- respond with: the 3-year
                  difference in term commitment at $12K/month is $432K in
                  flexibility value. For a growth company, that optionality
                  is worth the $5/RSF premium.

  Recommended strategy for TechVenture:
    Lead with flexibility and growth optionality. TechVenture is a Series B
    company with 45 employees targeting 80 in 18 months. A 10-year commitment
    at 245 Park locks them into a space they'll outgrow. Position 250 Park
    as the smart scale-up choice with expansion options on the 19th floor
    (8,500 SF ROFR available).
```
