# LP Pitch Deck Outline Template

---

## TEMPLATE INSTRUCTIONS (strip before use)

This template produces the section-by-section structure for a CRE fund LP pitch deck. Each section includes content guidance, slide count, and language standards. The deck should work for an initial LP meeting (30-45 minutes) and also function as a leave-behind.

**Audience**: Institutional LPs (pensions, endowments, family offices, fund-of-funds). Assume they see 200+ decks per year. Differentiation comes from specificity and credibility, not design.

**Total slide count**: 25-35 slides. Under 25 looks thin. Over 35 means you are padding or not editing. The appendix can be longer -- LPs will pull slides from the appendix for their IC process.

**Design rules**: Clean, professional. No stock photos. No clip art. No gradients. Dark text on light background. One data point per slide maximum. If a slide makes two points, split it into two slides.

### Slide 1: Title Slide

| Element | Content |
|---------|---------|
| Fund name | [Fund Name, LP] |
| Subtitle | [Strategy in 6-8 words: e.g., "Value-Add Multifamily in Northeast Secondary Markets"] |

| Confidentiality notice | "CONFIDENTIAL -- For Qualified Purchasers Only" |
| Contact | [GP Name, Email, Phone] |

**Do not include**: Logos of portfolio companies, awards, certifications. Save credibility signals for later.

### Slide 2: Firm Overview (1 slide)

| Element | Guidance |
|---------|---------|
| Firm name and year founded | Establishes tenure |
| AUM / assets managed | Current and historical peak |
| Number of investments | Deals closed, not deals evaluated |
| Team size | Investment professionals only; do not inflate with admin |
| Offices | Physical presence signals commitment to markets |
| One-sentence strategy | "We acquire [type] in [markets] at [basis] and create value through [mechanism]." |

### Slide 3: Why This Fund, Why Now (1 slide)

**The most important slide in the deck.** This is the macro thesis that explains why this strategy generates alpha right now. Structure:

1. **Market dislocation or inefficiency**: What structural condition creates the opportunity? (e.g., "Institutional capital has retreated from secondary-market MF, creating a 150-200bps cap rate premium vs. gateway markets with comparable fundamentals.")
2. **Why the GP is positioned**: What gives you an edge? (e.g., "Our Newark-focused sourcing network has produced off-market deal flow at an average 15% discount to marketed pricing across Fund I.")
3. **Time sensitivity**: Why must LPs commit now? (e.g., "The dislocation window is 18-24 months as institutional capital returns; Fund II deployment target is 12-18 months.")

## Section 2: Investment Strategy (4-5 slides)

### Slide 4: Strategy Summary (1 slide)

One-page strategy summary. Table format:

| Parameter | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Strategy | [Value-add / Core-plus / Opportunistic / Debt] |
| Property type | [MF / Office / Industrial / Diversified] |
| Geography | [Specific markets -- not "Nationwide" or "Sun Belt"] |
| Asset size | [$ range per deal] |
| Target hold period | [X-X years] |
| Target levered IRR (net) | [X-X%] |
| Target equity multiple (net) | [X.X-X.Xx] |
| Target fund size | $[X]M |
| GP commitment | [X]% of fund size |

### Slide 5: Target Markets (1 slide)

Map or table of target markets with selection criteria. For each market, show:
- Population growth (5yr)
- Job growth (5yr)
- Median household income and rent-to-income ratio
- Supply pipeline (units under construction as % of inventory)
- Vacancy rate vs. long-term average
- Cap rate spread vs. gateway markets

**Do not**: List 15 markets. You cannot have an edge in 15 markets. 3-5 markets with deep knowledge beats broad coverage.

### Slide 6: Acquisition Criteria (1 slide)

Specific, falsifiable criteria. LPs should be able to look at a deal and immediately know whether it fits.

| Criterion | Threshold |
|-----------|----------|
| Property type | [Specific] |
| Year built | [Range] |
| Unit count / SF | [Min-Max] |
| Purchase price | $[Min]-$[Max] |
| Price per unit/SF | Below $[X] (below replacement cost) |
| Going-in cap rate | [Min]%+ |
| Occupancy at acquisition | [Min]%+ |
| Value-add renovation budget | $[X]-$[X] per unit |
| Target rent premium post-renovation | $[X]+/month |
| Market vacancy | Below [X]% |
| Supply pipeline | Below [X]% of inventory |

### Slide 7: Value Creation Playbook (1-2 slides)

Step-by-step process for how you create value. Specificity matters. Not "we renovate units and increase rents" but:

1. **Acquire below replacement cost** -- Target $[X]/unit basis vs. $[X]/unit replacement
2. **Interior renovation** -- $[X]/unit spend on [kitchen, bath, flooring, fixtures]
3. **Rent repositioning** -- Capture $[X]/month premium supported by [X] comparable renovations
4. **Expense optimization** -- RUBS implementation, insurance rebid, property tax appeal
5. **Operational improvement** -- On-site management upgrade, maintenance response time SLA
6. **Strategic exit** -- Sell stabilized asset at [X.X]% cap to [buyer type] at [X]-year hold

For each step, cite a specific example from your track record if available.

### Slides 9-12: Deal Case Studies (3-4 slides, 1 per deal)

Each case study on one slide. Structure:

| Element | Content |
|---------|---------|
| Property photo | Actual photo, not rendering |
| Property summary | [Name], [Units/SF], [Market], [Vintage] |
| Acquisition | Date, price, price/unit, going-in cap, acquisition source |

**If you have losses, include one.** LPs respect GPs who acknowledge losses and explain what they learned. Hiding losses destroys credibility when they surface in diligence.

### Slide 13: Attribution Analysis (1 slide)

Show what drove returns. Decompose gross returns into components:

| Component | Contribution to Gross IRR |
|-----------|--------------------------|
| NOI growth (same-store) | [X]% |
| Renovation rent premium | [X]% |
| Cap rate compression | [X]% |
| Leverage effect | [X]% |
| Other (fee income, etc.) | [X]% |
| **Total Gross IRR** | **[X]%** |

This tells LPs whether returns are repeatable. NOI-growth-driven returns are more sustainable than cap-compression-driven returns.

| Section | Time | Notes |

| Strategy | 8 min | |

If you are losing the LP's attention by Slide 10, the thesis is not compelling enough. Do not proceed to terms; go back to the value proposition.

## Language Standards

### Use

- "The fund targets a net IRR of 12-15% and a net equity multiple of 1.7-2.0x."
- "Fund I generated a 14.2% net IRR and 1.85x net EM on $32M of invested equity, with 3 of 5 investments fully realized."
- "The GP is committing $2.5M (5% of target fund size), funded from personal capital."

### Avoid

- "We expect strong returns." (What returns? How strong?)
- "The market offers attractive opportunities." (Which market? What opportunities?)
- "Our team has deep experience." (Quantify: years, deals, dollars.)
- "We believe this is a unique strategy." (Every GP says this. Prove uniqueness with data.)
- "Returns have historically been attractive." (Past performance caveat required. Be specific about which returns.)
